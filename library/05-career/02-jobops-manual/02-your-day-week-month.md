# Your Day, Week, Month

A pipeline you have to think about is a pipeline you'll abandon by August. This chapter is the whole operating rhythm, sized honestly.

## Daily (5 minutes, or 25–30 when you apply)

Morning, phone: glance at any new `[lead]` issues from the overnight scout runs. Most days: nothing worth pursuing, done in ninety seconds. Comment on anything interesting.

At the laptop, when you open a session in `job-ops/`, Claude runs `scripts/followups.mjs` first and tells you if any follow-up is due — before anything else, because a due follow-up on a founder email is worth more than a new cold application (the book's data: replies disproportionately come on touch two).

When a lead is worth it: *"run the full pipeline for {company}, posting: {link}"* and give it the five touchpoints from chapter 1. One good application beats three rushed ones — the grading exists precisely so you don't feel guilty about the postings you skip.

## Weekly (Sunday, 10 minutes)

Say `/outcome weekly review`. You get three things: overdue and upcoming follow-ups with drafts ready; what's converting, by channel and framing, in honest counts ("founder emails: 2 replies from 5; form-only: 0 from 4") — not percentages pretending eleven data points are statistics; and proposed recalibrations, which only happen after you agree. This is also when confirmed learnings get promoted into permanent rules and stale ones get deleted — chapter 4 covers the mechanics.

## Monthly (the 1st — this one has a clock on it)

The HN *Who is Hiring* thread posts on the 1st and visibility decays within hours. The workflow will catch it automatically on its next 6-hour tick, but on the 1st it's worth being manual and early: run the scout yourself, grade immediately, and consider posting in the companion *Who wants to be hired* thread — the format (quantified outcome first, portfolio link first, narrow named specialization) is encoded in the outreach skill.

Also monthly: `node gh-tools/profile-audit.mjs`. Your GitHub is the first link every recruiter clicks, and the audit's fix list is ordered by what they see first. The first run already found real problems — flagships not pinned, FocuClone with no README at all, live URLs missing from repo headers. Thirty minutes of README polish is worth more than one extra application.

## Seasonally

After each YC Demo Day (spring and fall): freshly funded companies convert investor money into headcount within weeks. That's the highest-density window on Work at a Startup all year — push the target list hard, and remember the board is a *directory of founders to email*, not a place to click Apply.

## The two habits that compound

Everything above is mechanical except two things. First: **log every outcome**, even "silence, 14 days" — it's thirty seconds and it's the only food the learning loop eats. Second: **feed your profile** — every time you write an answer about yourself anywhere (a form, an interview prep, a late-night note), it goes into `profile/stories.md`. The system can only be as specific as the truth you've given it.
