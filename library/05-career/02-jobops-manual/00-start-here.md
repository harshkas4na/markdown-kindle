# Start Here

*Get Hired in 2026* told you what works: founder emails over job-board forms, specificity over volume, day-one applications, one checkable artifact in every message. JobOps is that book compiled into software. It lives at `PROJECTS/Me/job-ops`, and this manual is about operating it — not building it, running it.

## The one idea underneath everything

Every mass-apply tool ever built fails the same way: it scales the *sending* and destroys the *specificity*, and specificity is the only thing that converts. Wonsulting shut down its bulk auto-apply after clients averaged one interview per fifty sends. JobOps inverts that: it scales everything *around* the send — finding, researching, drafting, scoring, form-filling — and refuses, by design, to send anything itself. You are the send button. That's not a limitation you tolerate; it's the strategy.

## What you own vs what the system owns

The system owns the mechanical work: watching HN every six hours, building company dossiers, looping a resume until it scores 80+, drafting outreach in your voice, filling forms. You own exactly three things:

1. **The truth.** `profile/facts.yaml`, `stories.md`, `voice.md` — every generated word traces back to these three files. When they're stale, everything downstream is stale. When you answer a new question about yourself anywhere, it belongs in `stories.md`.
2. **The judgment.** Which companies matter, whether a dossier smells right, and the final ten minutes of editing before anything is sent. Chapter 2 maps the five exact moments the system stops and waits for you.
3. **The outcomes.** Telling the system what happened — a reply, a rejection, silence. Thirty seconds per event. Chapter 4 explains why this is the input the whole learning loop runs on, and why skipping it is how the system stays dumb forever.

## How to read this manual

Chapter 1 walks one application start to finish so you can see the machine move. Chapter 2 is the one to internalize — your daily, weekly, and monthly routine. Chapter 3 covers the GitHub layer: the part that runs while your laptop is closed and buzzes your phone. Chapter 4 is the learning loop. Chapter 5 is the psychology every generated message is engineered around — read it once even if you skip everything else, because it's also training for the live conversations no tool can attend for you.
