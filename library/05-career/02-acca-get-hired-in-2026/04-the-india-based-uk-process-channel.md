# The India-Based UK-Process Channel

If the visa math in chapter 3 rules out "move to the UK now," the next best question is: where can real, checkable UK-client work be done from India, with no visa involved? This is the channel that actually answers the "UK experience" question honestly, rather than trying to argue your way around it with exam results alone — because a Grant Thornton UK job posting found during this research spells out exactly how UK employers phrase the requirement: a professional qualification (ACA, ICAS, CA, ACCA, or CIPFA) *with post-qualification UK experience*. That specific phrase — UK experience, not just UK qualification — is what this channel is built to give you honestly, years before physical relocation is realistic.

## A necessary caveat before any names

Research into exactly which India-based firms actively hire ACCA part-qualified or affiliate candidates for UK-process work was only partially verifiable in the time available — some company career pages didn't load usable content, and several well-known names in this space couldn't be freshly confirmed. Treat everything below as a set of leads worth checking directly, not a confirmed hiring pipeline. That said, the category itself is real and well established in the Indian accounting job market, even where a specific firm's current openings couldn't be pulled.

## UK-process outsourcing firms

**QX Global Group** (qxglobalgroup.com) is a real, currently active firm that explicitly describes serving US, UK, and Ireland mid-market clients from delivery centres in India and Mexico. Its careers page doesn't explicitly name ACCA, so don't treat "they hire ACCA candidates" as confirmed — but the client mix is exactly the kind of UK-process work (VAT, UK GAAP/IFRS reporting, UK-based mid-market clients) that would produce genuinely checkable "UK experience" bullets if a role there is available. Worth a direct look at current openings.

**Meru Accounting** is a real firm whose careers page references work for "global businesses" without India/UK specifics confirmed in this research pass — again, worth checking directly rather than assuming.

One name worth explicitly ruling out: **TOA Global**, sometimes mentioned in this space, is confirmed Philippines-based and explicitly US/Canada-focused in its own materials — not a UK-process, India-based path. Don't spend time chasing this one.

## Big Four and mid-tier Global Delivery Centres in India

EY GDS, Deloitte USI, KPMG Global Services, PwC's Acceleration Centers, Grant Thornton INDUS, and similar delivery-centre operations run large-scale UK and US client tax, audit, and accounting work out of India — this is common knowledge in the Indian accounting job market, and these centres are well-known, large-volume employers of part-qualified and affiliate ACCA candidates for UK and US engagement work. This research pass couldn't pull a live, confirmed ACCA-specific hiring page for any of them in the time available, which is a gap worth closing with a direct search of each name plus "India" plus "ACCA" — but the category itself (Big Four GDC, UK-client engagement, India-based) is real, large, and a natural target list independent of what this book could verify.

## Why this channel matters beyond "a job"

Three concrete reasons to prioritize this over a direct UK application right now:

1. **It produces true "UK experience" bullets.** "Prepared VAT returns and CT600 filings for UK clients" is a real, checkable UK-client claim in a way "passed ATX-UK" isn't — and it's exactly the phrase the Grant Thornton posting (and, per pattern, most UK postings asking for "UK experience") is actually screening for.
2. **It's a natural home for PER.** If chapter 2's supervisor question gets solved at a firm like this — an IFAC-qualified manager or the firm's own external accountants willing to sign off Performance Objectives — the work itself (UK-process tax and reporting) maps directly onto PER's technical objectives, rather than needing to be retrofitted from unrelated bookkeeping work.
3. **It's a resume-honest path, not a workaround.** Unlike the current two-resume split, this channel lets both halves of the real story — UK tax/IFRS credential depth and hands-on cross-jurisdiction compliance work — sit in the same job at the same time, rather than forcing a choice between them.

## The practical move

Search LinkedIn and Naukri for "UK process" combined with "accounts," "UK tax," or "UK payroll" — this is a recognized, distinct category of posting in the Indian outsourcing job market, separate from generic "US process" roles, and one worth explicitly filtering for given how the current resumes have drifted toward the US-process framing by default. Pair any outreach here with the direct question this chapter opened with: does this role work UK clients specifically, and is there a qualified accountant on staff who could serve as a PER supervisor. Getting both answers before accepting anything is worth more than getting either answer alone.
