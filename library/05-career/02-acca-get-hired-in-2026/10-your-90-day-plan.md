# Your 90-Day Plan

Everything in this book fails if it stays theoretical. Here's the plan in a shape that actually runs — three tracks in parallel (PER/credentials, resume/outreach, exam prep), because running any one of them in isolation for three months is the most common way this goes nowhere.

## Before week 1: the two things everything else depends on

1. **The PER-supervisor check (chapter 2).** Before anything else, find out whether anyone connected to Mercurius Advisory or S.S. Soin & Co. was IFAC-body qualified and willing to sign off Performance Objectives, and confirm whether the Ethics and Professional Skills Module is already complete. This determines whether the last ten months count toward the 36-month clock at all — everything downstream is more effective once this is answered.
2. **One unified resume (chapter 8)**, built from the true combined summary, with target-specific reordering ready for both the UK-process/GDC framing and the cross-border-tax framing — replacing both existing PDFs.

## The weekly cadence (repeat for ~12 weeks)

**PER and credentials track**
- Week 1–2: log whatever experience can honestly be logged in MyExperience now; line up a PER supervisor per chapter 2, even if that means reaching out to three or four former contacts before finding one who says yes.
- Ongoing: register for and begin studying toward the Enrolled Agent exam (chapter 6), targeting the September–February international testing window; treat this as a fixed-deadline track that doesn't get quietly deprioritized behind SBL revision.
- Ongoing: SBL exam prep on whatever schedule matches the next available ACCA sitting — check the exact registration deadline on the ACCA portal directly rather than assuming.

**Resume and outreach track — aim for 5–8 targeted contacts/week across both channels**
- India-based UK-process outsourcing firms and Big Four/mid-tier GDCs (chapter 4): 3–5 targeted messages a week using the chapter 9 template, prioritizing any firm where a UK-client-facing role and a plausible PER supervisor might both exist.
- US-UK cross-border tax specialist firms and the wider global-mobility category (chapter 5): 2–3 targeted messages a week, leading with the US-side proof per chapter 9's second template.
- Track replies in a simple spreadsheet — company, channel, date, response, next step. If a channel's response rate stays low for several weeks running, that's a signal to sharpen the message's specificity, not to send more of the same version.

## Milestones

- **End of week 2:** unified resume finished, PER-supervisor conversation started (even if not yet resolved), EA study plan and target exam window confirmed.
- **End of week 4:** PER-supervisor question should have a real answer — yes, no, or "found someone new" — and at least 15–20 targeted outreach messages sent across both channels combined, with a few real replies logged.
- **End of week 8:** ideally one first-round conversation from either channel, and visible EA study progress against the September–February window. If the cross-border channel is converting notably better or worse than the UK-process channel, that's real signal about which of chapter 1's framings is landing harder right now — let the response data update the emphasis, not the other way around.
- **End of week 12:** re-run this plan for another cycle as needed, but revisit chapter 1's framework explicitly at this point. Three months of real response data across two channels, plus a resolved PER-supervisor situation and visible EA progress, is a far better input into how hard to lean into the cross-border bridge versus the UK-process channel than any single chapter in this book, including this one.

## The one rule that overrides everything else here

If a week gets busy and something has to give, protect the PER-supervisor question and the EA testing-window deadline before protecting outreach volume — those two have hard, external clocks attached (a three-year experience-recall problem in one case, a fixed annual testing window in the other), while outreach can absorb a slow week without lasting damage. Everything else in this book is a framing and positioning question that can be revisited and adjusted; those two are the only parts of the plan with a real deadline that doesn't wait.
