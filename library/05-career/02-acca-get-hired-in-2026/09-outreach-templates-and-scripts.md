# Outreach Templates and Scripts

Every message shape referenced earlier in the book, collected here. None of these are copy-paste — the bracketed detail is the entire point of each one; a filled-in template with the brackets left generic reads as mail-merge and gets ignored, especially in a field like accounting where precision is the actual job being evaluated.

## Cold message to a UK-process outsourcing firm or India-based GDC (Chapter 4)

> Subject: ACCA (11/12), UK + US tax background — [role/team] at [Company]
>
> Hi [Name] — I'm ACCA part-qualified (11 of 12 papers passed, including Advanced Taxation – UK and Strategic Business Reporting), based in Delhi NCR, currently working on [SBL / PER]. I've spent the last [X months] handling IFRS-based reporting and multi-currency bookkeeping for international clients, alongside hands-on US tax compliance work — ITIN processing, multi-state entity registration, IRS liaison. I noticed [Company] works UK-process client accounts from India, which lines up closely with where I want to build real UK-client experience next. Would a short call make sense before [role] closes? Happy to share more detail on the ATX-UK/SBR technical background or the compliance work either way.
>
> [Name] / [LinkedIn]

**Use when:** reaching a partner, manager, or recruiter at a firm confirmed (or plausibly) doing UK-process work from India — chapter 4's leads, or any similar firm found independently.

## Cold message to a US-UK cross-border tax specialist firm (Chapter 5)

> Subject: ACCA + hands-on US tax compliance — interested in [role] at [Company]
>
> Hi [Name] — I'm ACCA part-qualified (ATX-UK and SBR passed) with real hands-on US tax compliance experience: 50+ ITIN applications processed, LLC/corporation registration and dissolution across seven US states, EIN registration, and direct IRS and state-authority liaison. I'm working toward pairing that with [an Enrolled Agent designation / SBL and full ACCA membership], because the US-UK cross-border combination is exactly where I want to build a career, and [Company]'s work in this space is why I'm reaching out directly rather than just applying cold. Would you be open to a short conversation about where a candidate at this stage could realistically add value on your team?
>
> [Name] / [LinkedIn]

**Use when:** reaching firms like the ones named in chapter 5 — lead with the US-side proof first here, since that's the half these firms' own postings actually screen for; the ACCA credential and EA progress come in as the differentiator, not the headline.

## PER-supervisor request script (Chapter 2)

> Hi [Name] — hope you're doing well. I'm finishing up my ACCA exams (11 of 12 done, SBL remaining) and I'm working on my Practical Experience Requirement. I did [X months] at [Mercurius Advisory / S.S. Soin & Co.] under your [management / oversight], covering [specific work — IFRS reporting / ITIN processing / entity registration / bank reconciliations]. Would you be willing to review that work against ACCA's Performance Objectives and sign off as my PER supervisor, or point me to someone at the firm who could? Happy to put together a short summary of the specific work to make the review quick on your end.
>
> [Name]

**Use when:** reaching a former manager or colleague who might be IFAC-body qualified, per chapter 2's action items — send this before assuming the answer is no, and send it regardless of how long ago the role ended; ACCA's own guidance doesn't require the sign-off to happen while still employed there.

## Informational-interview request into global mobility or cross-border tax (Chapter 5)

> Hi [Name] — I've been building toward a US-UK cross-border tax specialism (ACCA, ATX-UK/SBR passed, hands-on US ITIN and multi-state tax compliance experience) and noticed you work in [global mobility / expatriate tax] at [Company]. Would you have 15 minutes to talk through how you got into this specifically, and what the realistic entry points look like for someone at my stage? Genuinely just trying to learn from someone actually doing the work before I commit further time to this direction.

**Use when:** first contact with someone in the niche you don't know at all — lowest pressure, and per chapter 7's honest counter-voice on how niche this specialism can become, a real conversation here is worth more than another job-board search before deciding how far to commit.

## Rules that apply to every template above

- **Lead with something specific and checkable**, not a generic claim — "50+ ITIN applications processed" resolves itself; "strong US tax knowledge" doesn't.
- **State your ACCA status precisely** — "11/12 papers passed, SBL remaining" or "affiliate, PER in progress" reads as someone who understands the qualification's actual mechanics, which chapter 7 showed plenty of employers themselves don't.
- **Never send the same filled-in message to twenty people.** Accounting and tax is a small enough field, especially in a specialist niche like cross-border tax, that a generic version circulating risks being noticed by more than one recipient.
- **One follow-up after about a week is normal**; more than that reads as pressure rather than interest.
