# One Resume, Not Two

Back to the two PDFs from chapter 0. The instinct behind them wasn't wrong — tailoring a resume per target, leading with different strengths for different audiences, is genuinely good practice (chapter 1 recommends exactly that). What went wrong is that these two weren't built as two *targeted* versions of one true story — they were built by deleting different halves of it. The CV version never mentions ITIN processing, EIN registration, or the IRS at all. The Resume version never mentions IFRS, cross-border regulatory compliance, or entity formation across multiple jurisdictions. Each one independently throws away the one thing that makes this profile unusual: not either half, but the combination.

## The unified base

Every version, every target, should start from one true summary that contains both halves, then reorder or lightly re-emphasize from there — never delete either side again. Something close to:

> Part-qualified ACCA professional (11 of 12 papers passed, including Advanced Taxation – UK and Strategic Business Reporting) with hands-on cross-border tax and compliance experience spanning US ITIN processing, multi-state US entity registration, and direct IRS and state authority liaison, alongside IFRS-based financial reporting and multi-currency bookkeeping for international clients.

That single sentence is the pitch nobody else in a typical UK-process or cross-border-tax applicant pool can make honestly — and it's the sentence both old resumes independently threw half of away.

## One achievements list, not two

Employee of the Month at Mercurius Advisory (February 2024), the ATX-UK pass, the SBR pass, and the 50+ ITIN applications are currently split across two separate documents. They belong in one list, together — a reader deciding whether to shortlist a candidate should see the full shape of what's been done in one pass, not half of it depending on which file happened to land in their inbox.

## What's missing from both resumes entirely

Neither current document mentions PER status, the Ethics and Professional Skills Module, or (once started, per chapter 6) Enrolled Agent progress. Adding a precise line on ACCA status — not just "part-qualified," but something like "11/12 papers passed; PER in progress" once that's genuinely true — signals to anyone in accounting who reads resumes for a living that the affiliate/PER distinction is understood, not glossed over. Chapter 7 showed real employers themselves are often unclear on this exact distinction; being precise about it is a small, free credibility signal that costs nothing and very few competing applicants bother to include.

## Reordering per target, from the same true document

- **UK-process outsourcing firm or India-based Big Four/mid-tier Global Delivery Centre role (chapter 4):** IFRS, ATX-UK, SBR, and the multi-currency/cross-border compliance bullets lead. The ITIN/EIN/IRS bullets stay in, positioned lower, as evidence of regulator-facing complexity handled well — not the headline here.
- **US-UK cross-border or expatriate tax specialist firm (chapter 5):** ITIN, EIN, multi-state registration, and IRS/state-authority liaison bullets lead. ATX-UK and SBR sit immediately behind them as the credential half of the same pitch — this is the one target where the combination itself is the entire point of the application, and it should read that way from the first line.
- **Anywhere else, when in doubt:** lead with the unified summary above unedited, and let the reader see both halves before deciding what matters most to them — better than guessing wrong and hiding the half that would have actually landed.

## LinkedIn

The LinkedIn headline and About section should mirror the unified summary, not either old resume's narrower framing — LinkedIn is usually the first thing a recruiter or hiring manager checks after a resume lands, and right now it's very likely echoing whichever of the two documents was sent most recently rather than presenting the combined story consistently across every channel at once.
