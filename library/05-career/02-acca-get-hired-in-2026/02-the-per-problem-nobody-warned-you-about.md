# The PER Problem Nobody Warned You About

Finishing SBL will feel like the finish line. It isn't. ACCA's own guidance is explicit: full membership requires three separate components, not one — all exams, the Ethics and Professional Skills Module (a separate online module from the thirteen exam papers — worth checking this week whether it's already done, since neither resume mentions it), and the Practical Experience Requirement: 36 months of relevant, supervised work experience plus nine signed-off Performance Objectives, five mandatory "Essentials" and four selectable "Technical" ones, logged through the MyExperience portal inside MyACCA. Passing every paper, SBL included, makes you an ACCA *affiliate* — not a member, and not entitled to describe yourself as ACCA-qualified until PER is done too. This is stated plainly on ACCA's own site, but it's the single most under-explained mechanic in the whole qualification, and it's the thing both resumes currently say nothing about.

## The good news: sector isn't the gate

ACCA's guidance on completing PER is more permissive than most candidates assume: "part-time positions, partially relevant roles, internships, and volunteering" all count, across "any sector." That's a meaningful, specific answer to a worry you might reasonably have — that ten months at a bookkeeping and tax outsourcing firm somehow doesn't "count" toward becoming a real ACCA member because it isn't a traditional UK or Big Four practice job. It counts, in principle. The Mercurius Advisory role, ITIN processing and all, is exactly the kind of relevant experience the PER framework is built to recognize.

## The bad news: supervision is the actual gate

Here's the part that actually determines whether that ten months turns into real PER credit or evaporates: a PER supervisor has to be "a qualified accountant" — a member of an IFAC member body, or someone recognized by law in your country as qualified. If your direct manager at the time wasn't one of those, ACCA's own process still allows a second, qualified supervisor to sign off the Performance Objectives instead — this can be another manager within the organization, an external consultant, or the organization's external accountants or auditors, as long as they have a genuine business connection to the employer. What it can't be is nobody. And this is a today problem, not a someday problem: **find out this week whether anyone connected to Mercurius Advisory — or, failing that, someone connected to your earlier internship at S.S. Soin & Co. — was IFAC-body qualified and willing to review the actual work and sign off.** If the answer is no, you need an external qualified accountant lined up now, before more unlogged, unverifiable months pass and the paper trail gets harder to reconstruct.

The reason this can't wait for "more experience to accumulate first" is a real thread from someone who found out too late. On OpenTuition, a candidate — going by "Mattia" — finished all their exams in January 2023 with genuinely qualifying work experience already behind them, only to discover neither their current manager nor their former manager would agree to sign off as a PER supervisor. ACCA's own response, per the thread, was essentially "find someone yourself." The thread ends without a resolution posted. The lesson isn't that ACCA will bail you out if you ask — it's that this is a self-directed problem, and the earlier you find out you have it, the more real options exist to fix it (a former colleague who's since qualified, an external accountant willing to review the work retrospectively, a next employer chosen partly because a qualified supervisor is actually available).

## Why "affiliate" doesn't buy you as much as it should

Even once every exam and Performance Objective is done, real accounts suggest the affiliate-to-member gap matters more in practice than the ACCA marketing implies. One Reddit thread on r/ACCA describes an employer telling an affiliate they were still being treated as part-qualified for pay purposes, and would need to "finish my 3 years quota to get a hike" regardless of having passed every exam. A career-advice site (ikkaclasses.com) puts the underlying problem plainly: "Some companies don't distinguish between ACCA affiliates and part-qualified students. Others assume full membership is already complete." Two different employers can read the exact same status two completely different ways — which means it's worth clarifying proactively, in a cover letter or interview, rather than assuming the reader already understands what "affiliate" means. And on OpenTuition, a London-based candidate who'd passed every one of the fourteen papers (under the older syllabus numbering) with zero work experience was told by a recruiter commenting on the thread to expect roughly £25,000 a year for an entry-level assistant-accountant role — "real crap" pay, in the recruiter's own words, but necessary just to start the PER clock. The thread's most-quoted line is worth sitting with: **"Part qualified means little these days as there is no consistency of use."** The exams were never going to be the differentiator on their own. What happens after them — PER, specifically — is.

## What to actually do about it, this week

1. Confirm whether the Ethics and Professional Skills Module is complete, separately from the thirteen exam papers.
2. Find out whether anyone at Mercurius Advisory (or S.S. Soin & Co.) held an IFAC-body qualification and would review the actual work and sign Performance Objectives, even retrospectively.
3. If not, start identifying an external qualified accountant — someone with a real business connection to a current or past employer — who could serve as a second supervisor going forward.
4. Log whatever can honestly be logged in MyExperience now. Waiting until "more experience" piles up before starting the paperwork is precisely the trap Mattia's thread illustrates — the backlog only gets harder to reconstruct, not easier.

None of this changes the framing question from chapter 1. But it's the structural fix that has to run in parallel with it — a perfectly positioned resume sent to exactly the right cross-border tax firm still won't clear a "must be ACCA qualified" filter if PER hasn't actually started counting.
