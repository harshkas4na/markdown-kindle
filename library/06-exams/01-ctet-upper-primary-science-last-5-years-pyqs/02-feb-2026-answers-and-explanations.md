# February 2026 — Answers and Explanations

Answers below are taken from the official CBSE final answer key for the 08 February 2026 session, Set G (Paper II). Where the key marked an erratum (multiple accepted options), this is noted explicitly. Explanations are original teaching notes meant to build transferable understanding, not paraphrases of any answer key.

## Part I: Child Development and Pedagogy

**Q1.** When do students learn most efficiently and meaningfully?
**Correct answer: (2) — their responses and subjective experiences are attended and valued.**
Meaningful learning happens when learners feel heard and their own experiences are treated as legitimate starting points for new knowledge — this is the core humanist and constructivist idea that learning is built on, not poured into, the learner. The other options describe exactly the opposite conditions: disconnected content, incoherent information, and fear of failure all suppress engagement rather than support it.

**Q2.** Where should adaptations be made in an inclusive classroom?
**Correct answer: (2) — (a), (b), (c), (d), i.e., learning goals, teaching-learning strategies, provisioning of support, and assessment.**
Inclusion is not a single accommodation bolted onto one part of teaching; it requires rethinking the whole instructional cycle — what is taught, how it is taught, what support is provided, and how learning is checked. Restricting adaptation to only some of these areas would leave gaps that exclude learners with diverse needs at some stage of the process.

**Q3.** Assertion-Reason on writing about emotions in middle school.
**Correct answer: (3) — Both (A) and (R) are true and (R) is the correct explanation of (A).**
Reflective writing about feelings requires the learner to label, examine, and make sense of an emotional experience — this is genuinely a cognitive act (not merely an emotional release), and that cognitive processing is exactly why it helps students act more thoughtfully afterward. Because reflection converts raw feeling into examined understanding, R directly explains why A is a sound classroom practice.

**Q4.** Assertion-Reason on avoiding collaboration and group work.
**Correct answer: (2) — Both (A) and (R) are false.**
Modern pedagogy (Vygotskian and constructivist alike) treats social interaction and collaborative talk as central engines of learning, not obstacles to avoid. Learning is also not a passive, rote, imitation-based process — active construction of meaning through dialogue and experience is what produces durable understanding, so both statements misrepresent how learning actually works.

**Q5.** Assertion-Reason on diversity and inclusion.
**Correct answer: (1) — (A) is true but (R) is false.**
Diversity and inclusion do improve teaching and learning by exposing students to multiple perspectives and richer classroom discourse. But conversing across differences does not hinder critical or creative thinking — it typically strengthens it, since learners must justify, compare, and re-examine their own views, so R is false even though A is correct.

**Q6.** Assertion-Reason on dyslexia and phonological processing.
**Correct answer: (4) — Both (A) and (R) are true but (R) is not the correct explanation of (A).**
It's factually accurate that dyslexia involves phonological-processing difficulty that makes decoding words hard, and it's also true that all children benefit from encouragement in their strengths. But the second statement is a general motivational principle, not an explanation of the specific neuro-cognitive mechanism behind dyslexia — so R doesn't explain why A is true.

**Q7.** Which statement about learning is NOT correct?
**Correct answer: (1) — Social interaction hinders learning.**
This is the false statement (which is what the question asks for): social interaction is one of the strongest supports for learning, not a hindrance, because it allows learners to negotiate meaning, get feedback, and see alternative viewpoints. The remaining three options — meaningful learning through real tasks, active knowledge construction, and building on existing understanding — are all well-established, accurate descriptions of how learning works.

**Q8.** Two commonly used problem-solving strategies.
**Correct answer: (3) — Heuristics and algorithms.**
Algorithms are step-by-step procedures guaranteed to produce a correct solution, while heuristics are flexible "rules of thumb" that narrow down possibilities faster but without a guarantee. Together they represent the two classic ends of the problem-solving spectrum taught in educational psychology — "response set" and "functional fixedness," by contrast, are obstacles to problem-solving, not strategies for it.

**Q9.** According to constructivist theorists.
**Correct answer: (3) — Children have a natural tendency to 'make sense' of the world.**
Constructivism holds that children are active meaning-makers who continuously build and revise mental models of how the world works, rather than passive recipients of ready-made information. The other options describe behaviourist or deficit views of the child (needing correction, needing external motivation, or absorbing pre-packaged facts) that constructivism explicitly rejects.

**Q10.** Effective teaching strategies for diverse learner needs.
**Correct answer: (1) — (a) and (c), i.e., Differentiated Instruction and Individualized Education Plans.**
Both differentiated instruction and IEPs are designed precisely to flex content, process, or pace to match individual learner profiles. A standardized curriculum does the opposite — it applies one fixed approach to everyone — so it cannot be listed as a strategy for meeting diverse needs.

**Q11.** Which is NOT an effective problem-solving strategy?
**Correct answer: (1) — Overlooking relevant information.**
Ignoring relevant information removes exactly the data needed to solve a problem correctly, making it a hindrance rather than a strategy. Breaking problems into parts, recognizing patterns, and using logical reasoning are all textbook components of systematic problem-solving.

**Q12.** Designing a lesson for mixed-ability learners, per Vygotsky.
**Correct answer: (1) — consider each student's current level of understanding and provide appropriate support to help them reach the next level.**
This is the essence of Vygotsky's Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD): learning is most effective when support (scaffolding) is pitched just above what a learner can do independently, tailored to where each learner currently stands. Focusing only on advanced students, making everyone work alone, or giving everyone identical challenge all ignore the very idea of a personalized ZPD.

**Q13.** Characteristics of "lacking reading fluency," "word reversal," "distractibility."
**Correct answer: (3) — Dyslexia.**
These three signs — disfluent reading, reversing letters/words, and difficulty sustaining attention while reading — are classic behavioural markers used to identify dyslexia, a specific learning difficulty rooted in phonological processing. They are not core diagnostic features of dementia, ADHD, or autism spectrum disorder, even though attention difficulties can co-occur across conditions.

**Q14.** Environment that motivates students to learn.
**Correct answer: (4) — (a) and (d), i.e., Belongingness and Safety.**
Motivation research (e.g., Maslow, self-determination theory) consistently shows that students engage and take intellectual risks only when they feel they belong and are physically/emotionally safe. Fear and rejection are demotivating conditions that push students toward avoidance, not engagement.

**Q15.** Helpful strategy for understanding new content.
**Correct answer: (3) — (a) and (b), i.e., rereading once, and asking questions/seeking clarification.**
Actively seeking clarification is a well-established comprehension-monitoring strategy, and a single rereading pass can help consolidate understanding when paired with active questioning. Pretending to understand and inventing private meanings for unfamiliar terms are exactly the passive, avoidant habits that prevent genuine comprehension.

**Q16.** Assertion-Reason on person-first language.
**Correct answer: (3) — Both (A) and (R) are true and (R) is the correct explanation of (A).**
Using person-first language ("student with disabilities" rather than "disabled student") is recommended precisely because language shapes how we perceive and value people — putting the person before the condition signals that the person is not defined by it. This is a direct cause-and-effect relationship, so R correctly explains why A is good practice.

**Q17.** Piaget's stage for hypothetical, abstract mental trial and error.
**Correct answer: (2) — Formal operational stage.**
The formal operational stage (roughly age 11+) is when adolescents can reason about abstract possibilities, form hypotheses mentally, and test them logically without needing concrete objects in front of them. The concrete operational stage still needs tangible referents; sensorimotor and pre-operational stages are far too early for hypothetical-deductive reasoning.

**Q18.** NEP 2020 on transforming assessment.
**Correct answer: (1) — incorporating self-assessment and peer-assessment.**
NEP 2020 explicitly pushes assessment reform toward continuous, competency-based approaches that involve learners in evaluating their own and peers' work, moving away from one-shot summative testing. The other options (unitary intelligence, ignoring socio-cultural context, pure summative assessment) run directly counter to NEP 2020's stated vision.

**Q19.** Piaget's definition of cognitive development.
**Correct answer: (2) — the process of adaptation to the environment by changing one's schemas.**
For Piaget, cognitive development is driven by the twin processes of assimilation and accommodation, through which existing mental structures (schemas) are adjusted to fit new experiences — this active restructuring is "adaptation." It is not a simple additive accumulation of facts, nor is it defined by cultural tools (that's more a Vygotskian idea) or dependent solely on language.

**Q20.** Vygotsky-based teacher gives hints/cues so students retry a problem.
**Correct answer: (2) — Scaffolding.**
Scaffolding is precisely this: providing temporary, tailored support (hints, cues, prompts) that helps a learner accomplish something just beyond their independent ability, which is gradually withdrawn as competence grows. Functional fixedness and assimilation describe unrelated cognitive phenomena, and collective monologue is a Piagetian term about young children talking without truly conversing.

**Q21.** Constructivist conditions that facilitate learning.
**Correct answer: (3) — Activities are interactive and collaborative.**
Constructivism holds that learners build knowledge through active engagement and social negotiation of meaning, so interactive, collaborative activities are the ideal condition. Teacher-centric, culturally indifferent, disorderly, or disconnected approaches are the opposite of what constructivist pedagogy recommends.

**Q22.** Kohlberg — moral reasoning of very young children.
**Correct answer: (3) — Ethics of egocentricism.**
In Kohlberg's pre-conventional stage, very young children reason about right and wrong mainly from their own immediate perspective (avoiding punishment, gaining reward for themselves), which is the "ethics of egocentricism." Ethics of others' perspectives, laws, or care reflect later, more socially oriented stages of moral development.

**Q23.** Which is NOT a sensory impairment?
**Correct answer: (4) — dysgraphia.**
Dysgraphia is a specific learning difficulty affecting the physical and cognitive process of writing — it is neurological/motor in nature, not a sensory impairment. Hearing loss, partial vision loss, and colour blindness are all impairments of the sensory organs (ears/eyes) themselves.

**Q24.** Assertion-Reason on delayed physical development affecting social-emotional development.
**Correct answer: (3) — Both (A) and (R) are true and (R) is the correct explanation of (A).**
Development across domains (physical, cognitive, social, emotional) is interconnected and continuous — a delay in one area can ripple into others because development builds cumulatively on itself. This principle of developmental continuity is exactly why a physical delay can genuinely affect social and emotional growth, so R explains A.

**Q25.** Shift from conservative to progressive education.
**Correct answer: (1) — performance-oriented goals to mastery-oriented goals.**
Progressive education emphasizes deep understanding and competence (mastery) over merely performing well for grades or approval, encouraging risk-taking and learning from mistakes. Shifting toward exam-centered pedagogy or standardized curricula would actually move education in the conservative direction, not the progressive one.

**Q26.** Rapid height/weight increase before sexual maturity in adolescence.
**Correct answer: (3) — Growth spurt.**
The adolescent growth spurt is the well-documented rapid acceleration in height and weight that typically precedes the onset of puberty's sexual maturation markers. Menstruation and pubescence refer to specific reproductive milestones, not the general physical growth acceleration itself.

**Q27.** Assertion-Reason on heredity, nutrition and exercise affecting height/build.
**Correct answer: (3) — Both (A) and (R) are true and (R) is the correct explanation of (A).**
Height and body build have a strong genetic component, but the environment (nutrition, exercise) can still meaningfully shape how that genetic potential is expressed. This gene-environment interaction is a foundational principle of developmental science and is exactly why R explains A.

**Q28.** Assertion-Reason on gendered portrayal of characters and school socialization.
**Correct answer: (3) — Both (A) and (R) are true and (R) is the correct explanation of (A).**
It is well documented that children's books often reproduce gender stereotypes (adventurous boys, nurturing girls), and schools/curricula are recognized "agents of secondary socialization" that transmit such gender-role expectations. Because curriculum materials actively shape how children understand gender roles, R correctly explains why A happens.

**Q29.** Focus of classroom motivation culture.
**Correct answer: (1) — understand and appreciate the process of learning.**
A mastery-oriented classroom culture centers students' motivation on genuinely engaging with and valuing the learning process itself, which builds intrinsic motivation and resilience. Motivating students through proving worth to others, avoiding shame, or competition instead breeds performance anxiety and surface-level engagement.

**Q30.** Role of peers in socialization.
**Correct answer: (2) — Peers have a greater influence on socialization during adolescence than during early childhood.**
As children grow into adolescence, peer groups become an increasingly powerful socializing force (identity formation, values, behaviour), overtaking some of the influence parents held in early childhood — though parents remain important throughout. Claiming peers have "no" or "minor" influence across all stages contradicts well-established developmental research.

## Part II: Mathematics and Science

**Q31.** Teaching-learning material for shapes, perimeter, area, polygons.
**Correct answer: (3) — Geoboard.**
A geoboard (a board with a grid of pegs and rubber bands) lets students physically construct and manipulate shapes to directly explore perimeter, area, and polygon properties, making it the most hands-on tool of the options for this specific topic. Cuisenaire rods are better suited to number/place-value work, Tangrams to shape composition/decomposition, and the Tower of Hanoi to logical/recursive problem-solving.

**Q32.** Complementary angles (2x−10°) and (3x−50°); find (4x+40°).
**Correct answer: (1) — 160°.**
Complementary angles sum to 90°, so (2x−10)+(3x−50)=90 gives 5x−60=90, so 5x=150 and x=30. Substituting, 4x+40 = 120+40 = 160°. The key algebraic rule here — complementary angles sum to 90°, supplementary to 180° — is one of the most frequently tested facts in geometry.

**Q33.** Statements about the project method in teaching mathematics.
**Correct answer: (2) — (a) and (c).**
The project method is fundamentally a learning-by-doing approach in which the learner takes an active, exploratory role — becoming a discoverer of mathematical ideas rather than a passive recipient. It does not minimize the learner's role, and it is not exclusively deductive; it typically encourages inductive discovery through hands-on investigation.

**Q34.** Tests comparing relative performance within a specific group.
**Correct answer: (3) — Norm referenced tests.**
Norm-referenced tests rank an individual's performance relative to a defined peer group (norms), which is exactly "relative performance of members of a specific group." Criterion-referenced tests instead measure performance against a fixed standard regardless of how others perform, and achievement/diagnostic tests serve different purposes altogether.

**Q35.** Purposes of formative assessment.
**Correct answer: (2) — (a) and (c).**
Formative assessment is assessment *for* learning — it is woven into daily teaching to give students ongoing feedback that helps them improve. Ranking and placement of children is a summative/selective function, not a formative one, so (b) does not belong.

**Q36.** Which measurements can form a right triangle?
**Correct answer: (3) — 14, 50, 48.**
Check the Pythagorean relation: 14² + 48² = 196 + 2304 = 2500 = 50². Only this triplet satisfies a² + b² = c²; the others fail the test, confirming that recognizing (or deriving) Pythagorean triples is the key skill being tested.

**Q37.** Renu's overall percentage gain selling articles A and B together.
**Correct answer: (2) — 18%.**
A 15% loss on the ₹382.50 selling price means the cost price of A was 382.50 ÷ 0.85 = ₹450; a 12% gain on ₹616 means the cost price of B was 616 ÷ 1.12 = ₹550. Total cost = ₹1,000, total selling price = ₹1,180, so overall profit = ₹180, i.e., 18% — the general rule is always to recover cost price from the loss/gain percentage before combining totals, never average the two percentages directly.

**Q38.** Why understanding patterns matters for teaching algebra.
**Correct answer: (1) — Patterns are associated with generalization.**
Algebra is fundamentally about expressing general relationships (a rule that works for every case), and spotting patterns is the cognitive bridge that lets students move from specific numeric instances to a general symbolic rule. The other options describe incidental benefits (creativity, ease, real-life use) rather than the core pedagogical reason patterns matter for algebra.

**Q39.** Cost of plastering floor and walls of a swimming pool.
**Correct answer: (4) — ₹24,360.**
Floor area = 20 × 15 = 300 m²; wall area = 2 × (20+15) × 4 = 280 m² (perimeter × depth, since the pool's inner walls are plastered to the given depth). Total area = 580 m², and at ₹42/m² the cost is 580 × 42 = ₹24,360 — remember that a swimming pool's "walls" for this kind of problem use the base perimeter, not the pool's total surface area formula for a closed box.

**Q40.** Difference between central angles for March and January sales in a pie chart.
**Correct answer: (3) — 27.2°.**
Total sold = 500+700+840+750+890+820 = 4,500, so each unit corresponds to 360°/4,500 = 0.08° per computer. March's angle = 840 × 0.08 = 67.2°, January's = 500 × 0.08 = 40°, and the difference is 27.2° — the key technique is finding degrees-per-unit once, then applying it, rather than recomputing full angles from scratch each time.

**Q41.** Which mathematics problem is inter-disciplinary in nature?
**Correct answer: (1) — Write your name and draw the lines of symmetry in each alphabet wherever possible.**
This task blends mathematics (symmetry) with language (letters of one's name), making it genuinely cross the boundary between two subject areas — that blending is what "inter-disciplinary" means pedagogically. The other three tasks stay entirely within geometry/symmetry and do not connect to another discipline.

**Q42.** Difference between Rajiv's and Amit's compound interest at maturity.
**Correct answer: (3) — ₹637.**
Rajiv: ₹7,000 at 20% p.a. compounded half-yearly for 1.5 years means a 10% half-yearly rate over 3 periods: 7000×(1.10)³ = ₹9,317, interest = ₹2,317. Amit: ₹8,000 at 10% p.a. compounded annually for 2 years: 8000×(1.10)² = ₹9,680, interest = ₹1,680. Difference = 2,317 − 1,680 = ₹637 — the recurring exam trap here is forgetting to halve the rate and double the number of periods when compounding is half-yearly.

**Q43.** Faces, vertices, edges of a triangular pyramid (tetrahedron).
**Correct answer: (3) — 2x+3y−2z=8.**
A triangular pyramid (tetrahedron) has 4 faces, 4 vertices, and 6 edges, so x=4, y=4, z=6. Substituting into option (3): 2(4)+3(4)−2(6) = 8+12−12 = 8, which checks out — this question is really testing Euler's formula (F+V−E=2) combined with basic knowledge of a tetrahedron's structure.

**Q44.** Best method of proof for √2 being irrational.
**Correct answer: (1) — Contradiction.**
The classical proof that √2 is irrational assumes the opposite (that it can be written as a fraction in lowest terms), and derives a logical contradiction from that assumption — this is the textbook "proof by contradiction" and remains the standard, most elegant method taught for this result. Induction, direct proof, and contrapositive are valid proof techniques in general but are not the natural fit for this particular irrationality proof.

**Q45.** Diagnosing a student's error in ordering integers.
**Correct answer: (4) — The student has understood the concept of arranging integers but has done a mistake by arranging them in increasing order.**
The student's answer (−150, −20, 0, 23, 46) is a perfectly correctly ordered sequence — just in ascending, not descending, order. This tells us the student knows how to compare and sequence integers correctly; the error is procedural (misreading "descending" as "ascending"), not conceptual, which is an important distinction for diagnosing student errors in teaching practice.

**Q46.** Congruence of triangles ABC and DBC given side measurements.
**Correct answer: (2) — ∆ABC ≅ ∆DCB, by SSS.**
With AB=5, AC=8, BC=4 for triangle ABC, and BD=8, CD=5 with the same base BC=4 for triangle DBC, matching sides are AB=CD=5, AC=BD=8, and the shared side BC — so the correspondence is A↔D, B↔C, C↔B, giving ∆ABC ≅ ∆DCB by the Side-Side-Side (SSS) congruence rule. Getting the vertex correspondence right (DCB, not DBC) is exactly what this question is testing.

**Q47.** Which number is NOT divisible by 72?
**Correct answer: (4) — 95142.**
72 = 8 × 9, so a number is divisible by 72 only if it's divisible by both 8 (last three digits divisible by 8) and 9 (digit sum divisible by 9). For 95142, the digit sum is 9+5+1+4+2=21, which is not divisible by 9, so it fails the test — the other three numbers pass both the 8-test and the 9-test.

**Q48.** Mean of the data given the median is 61.5.
**Correct answer: (2) — 59.3.**
With 10 ordered values, the median is the average of the 5th and 6th terms: (a + a+3)/2 = 61.5, giving 2a+3=123, so a=60 and a+3=63. The full data set (28,31,47,51,60,63,71,74,78,90) sums to 593, and 593/10 = 59.3 — the key idea is using the median formula for even-sized data sets to solve for the unknown before computing the mean.

**Q49.** A factor of 4x²+9y²+12xy−5(2x+3y)−14.
**Correct answer: (1) — 2x+3y−7.**
Notice 4x²+9y²+12xy is exactly (2x+3y)², so letting u=2x+3y, the expression becomes u²−5u−14, which factors as (u−7)(u+2). Substituting back gives the two factors (2x+3y−7) and (2x+3y+2) — spotting the perfect-square pattern inside a messier expression is the transferable algebra skill here.

**Q50.** Value of (3x+4) given 5(3x+4)−8(6x+7)=9x−8.
**Correct answer: (1) — 2.**
Expanding: 15x+20−48x−56 = 9x−8, so −33x−36 = 9x−8, giving −44 = ... let's solve carefully: −33x−36−9x=−8 → −42x=28 → x=−2/3. Then 3x+4 = 3(−2/3)+4 = −2+4 = 2 — always fully expand and collect like terms before substituting back into the expression the question actually asks for.

**Q51.** Best reason for a market field trip while teaching 'discount'.
**Correct answer: (4) — It would provide opportunity to students to see different advertisements related to 'sales' and to discuss with shopkeepers how to calculate it.**
Grounding an abstract concept like discount in a real, lived context (market advertisements, real shopkeeper calculations) is the pedagogically strongest justification — it connects mathematics to authentic, everyday problem-solving. "Enjoyment" or informal learning as ends in themselves, or claiming it as a mandatory NCF requirement, are not the substantive pedagogical reasons the question is testing for.

**Q52.** Value of (a−b) given a+b=17/6 and ab=2, a>b.
**Correct answer: (1) — 1/6.**
Using the identity (a−b)² = (a+b)² − 4ab = (17/6)² − 8 = 289/36 − 288/36 = 1/36, so a−b = ±1/6; since a>b, we take the positive root, 1/6 — this identity (a−b)²=(a+b)²−4ab is a standard algebraic shortcut worth memorizing for such problems.

**Q53.** Quadrilateral ABCD with AB∥DC, ∠B=70°, AB=CD.
**Correct answer: (2) — ∠A=110°, ∠C=110°.**
Since AB=CD with AB∥DC, ABCD is an isosceles trapezium, and in an isosceles trapezium the two angles at each parallel side are equal in a specific symmetric pattern: ∠A and ∠C (the "opposite" base angles across the legs) both work out to 110°, complementing the given 70° base angle at B along the same leg. This question tests the special angle properties of isosceles trapeziums, distinct from a general parallelogram.

**Q54.** Value of (8x+2¼), given product of two rationals is −40/3, one is −5/2, x is reciprocal of the other.
**Correct answer: (2) — 3¾.**
The other number = (−40/3) ÷ (−5/2) = (−40/3)×(−2/5) = 16/3, so x, its reciprocal, is 3/16. Then 8x + 2¼ = 8×(3/16) + 9/4 = 3/2 + 9/4 = 6/4+9/4 = 15/4 = 3¾ — the trap here is confusing "the other number" with "x" (which is explicitly defined as its reciprocal), so read such multi-step word problems carefully before computing.

**Q55.** Capacity (in litres) of a cylinder where height = 3 × base radius, base area = 616 cm².
**Correct answer: (4) — 25.872.**
From πr² = 616 with π=22/7, r² = 616×7/22 = 196, so r = 14 cm and height = 3×14 = 42 cm. Volume = base area × height = 616 × 42 = 25,872 cm³, and since 1 litre = 1,000 cm³, the capacity is 25.872 litres — always convert cm³ to litres at the very end, dividing by 1,000.

**Q56.** Which statements about numbers/squares/cubes/square roots are true?
**Correct answer: (2) — (d) only.**
The square root of a prime number is always irrational (never expressible as a ratio of integers), so (d) is true. But numbers between 0 and 1 are smaller than their squares (a number can be exceeded by its square, e.g., 0.25 < 0.5²... actually 0.5²=0.25<0.5, so fractions violate "never exceeds its square"), and similar counterexamples break (a), (b), and (c) — this question rewards testing each claim against fractions and negative numbers, not just whole numbers.

**Q57.** Value of an expression involving x/y defined via negative exponents.
**Correct answer: multiple options accepted (official key marks this item "Z" — all options treated as correct due to an error/ambiguity in the printed question).**
This item had a flaw in how it was set or printed, so CBSE awarded marks to every candidate regardless of the option chosen. The transferable exam skill it was aiming to test is still worth practicing: converting negative exponents to positive reciprocals (a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ) before simplifying compound fraction expressions.

**Q58.** Radius of C3 whose area equals sum of areas of C1 and C2.
**Correct answer: (1) — 52 m.**
From the perimeters, r1 = 40π/2π = 20 m and r2 = 96π/2π = 48 m. Since area of C3 = area of C1 + area of C2, we get r3² = r1² + r2² = 400+2304 = 2704, so r3 = 52 m — note this is structurally identical to the Pythagorean relationship, even though it's about circle areas, not triangle sides.

**Q59.** Numeration system clocks are based on.
**Correct answer: (3) — Sexagesimal System.**
Clocks divide time into 60 seconds per minute and 60 minutes per hour, which is the base-60 (sexagesimal) numbering system inherited from ancient Babylonian mathematics. It is a distinct system from the everyday base-10 (decimal), base-2 (binary), or base-16 (hexadecimal) systems.

**Q60.** Value of (√5043 × √50) / (√32 × √1323).
**Correct answer: (4) — 205/84.**
Simplify each radical: √5043 = √(3×41²) = 41√3, √50 = 5√2, √32 = 4√2, √1323 = √(441×3) = 21√3. The numerator becomes 41√3×5√2 = 205√6, and the denominator becomes 4√2×21√3 = 84√6; the √6 cancels, leaving 205/84 — factoring each radicand into a perfect square times a small remaining factor is the key simplification technique.

**Q61.** Attributes associated with 'Scientific temper'.
**Correct answer: (4) — (A), (B) and (C).**
Scientific temper includes perseverance in investigation, the creativity to break big questions into solvable parts, and healthy scepticism toward unfounded/non-scientific beliefs. "Commitment towards cultural preservation" is a valuable social attitude but has no intrinsic connection to the scientific method or scientific thinking, so it doesn't belong.

**Q62.** Are galvanization and electroplating physical or chemical changes?
**Correct answer: (3) — Process A is a physical change and B is a chemical change.**
Galvanization (commonly done by hot-dip coating with zinc) is treated here as depositing a protective physical layer, while electroplating is an electrolytic (redox) process — a chemical change driven by electric current that reduces metal ions onto a surface. This distinction (mechanical/physical deposition vs. electrochemical reaction) is the concept being tested.

**Q63.** Process skill a teacher develops by asking "why didn't the bowl sink? What will make it sink?"
**Correct answer: (3) — Hypothesizing.**
By prompting students to propose a tentative explanation ("why") and predict a testable outcome ("what will make it sink"), the teacher is pushing them to formulate hypotheses, not simply observe or infer passively. Observing is what students already did (noticing sinking/floating); the follow-up questions specifically invite hypothesis generation.

**Q64.** Which will NOT contribute to a science teacher's professional development?
**Correct answer: (1) — Regular reading of science content given in science textbooks.**
Merely re-reading the same textbook content a teacher already teaches offers little new professional growth, whereas visiting planetariums/museums, cultivating science-related hobbies, and networking with other teachers all expose a teacher to fresh ideas, tools, and practices. Professional development requires going beyond one's existing content knowledge, not repeating it.

**Q65.** Least suitable task for teaching science-society relationships.
**Correct answer: (1) — Concept map on sources of energy.**
A concept map on energy sources is a purely content-organization task focused on scientific facts, with no built-in link to societal impact, debate, or human contribution — unlike a poster on scientists' societal contributions, a debate on nuclear energy's social implications, or a project on technology's societal impact. The question specifically wants the task least connected to "science and society," and this one stays purely within the science content itself.

**Q66.** Classifying metals by their reactivity with water.
**Correct answer: (3) — Potassium, Iron, Copper.**
Potassium is one of the most reactive metals and reacts violently even with cold water; iron reacts only with steam (not cold water) to form iron oxide and hydrogen; copper is unreactive with water under normal conditions. This ordering directly reflects the standard reactivity series taught in middle-school chemistry.

**Q67.** Which is NOT done during waste-water treatment?
**Correct answer: (1) — Inorganic impurities such as nitrates and phosphates are removed by chlorination.**

**Q70.** Which wire produces least heat, given length and cross-sectional area?
**Correct answer: (1) — C.**

Resistance follows R∝L/A, so comparing L/A for each wire: A→10/10=1, B→10/8=1.25, C→12/6=2, D→5/10=0.5. **Q114.** Best description of 'procedural knowledge' in learning grammar.

Procedural knowledge is "knowing how" — the practical ability to actually apply a grammar rule in real use, not just to state or recognize it. **Q115.** Words we recognize on hearing/seeing them.
**Correct answer: (1) — Receptive vocabulary.**

**Q116.** The innateness hypothesis of language acquisition.

The innateness hypothesis (associated with Chomsky) proposes that humans are born with an inherent language faculty/universal grammar that predisposes them to acquire language naturally. **Q117.** Meaning of 'content-oriented language inputs.'
**Correct answer: (1) — language inputs that focus on information.**
Content-oriented inputs emphasize the meaning/information being conveyed (what is being communicated) rather than the linguistic form itself. This is the direct counterpart to "form-oriented" inputs, which instead focus on vocabulary, pronunciation, and grammar structures.

**Q118.** Educational philosophy advocating real-world/case-based learning over fixed instructional sequences.

Constructivism holds that learners build understanding best through authentic, real-world or case-based experiences rather than rigid, pre-set sequences of instruction — exactly matching the description given. **Q119.** Which does NOT come under institutional writing?

**Q126.** अध्ययन के द्वारा क्या प्राप्त किया जा सकता है, यह पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (1) — रुचियों का विस्तारण।**
गद्यांश स्पष्ट कहता है कि अध्ययन के द्वारा रुचियों को 'विस्तृत, जागृत और परिष्कृत' किया जा सकता है, जो सीधे 'रुचियों के विस्तारण' से मेल खाता है। शेष विकल्प गद्यांश के कथन के विपरीत या असंबंधित हैं (जैसे अंधविश्वास मिटाना बताया गया है, अधिग्रहण करना नहीं)।

**Q127.** 'समालोचना' शब्द का उपसर्ग की दृष्टि से सही विग्रह पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (3) — सम् + आलोचना।**
'समालोचना' में मूल उपसर्ग 'सम्' है जो 'आलोचना' शब्द से जुड़कर संधि के नियमानुसार 'समालोचना' बनता है (सम् + आलोचना = समालोचना)। अन्य विग्रह व्याकरणिक दृष्टि से गलत उपसर्ग-विभाजन प्रस्तुत करते हैं।

**Q128.** 'पढ़ने के साथ गुनना' का तात्पर्य पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (4) — पढ़े गए पर चिंतन मनन करना।**
गद्यांश में स्पष्ट किया गया है कि 'गुनना' का अर्थ है पढ़े गए विषय पर 'गहराई से चिंतन-मनन करना', न कि केवल याद करना या पुनर्लेखन करना। यह अध्ययन और रटंत ज्ञान के बीच के महत्वपूर्ण अंतर को रेखांकित करता है।

**Q129.** 'प्राचीन' (ancient) का सही विलोम पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (4) — नवीन।**
'प्राचीन' (पुराना) का सीधा विपरीतार्थक शब्द 'नवीन' (नया) है। 'भविष्य' समय-सूचक है न कि प्राचीन का विलोम, और 'अधीन'/'परंपरागत' का अर्थ भी भिन्न है।

**Q130.** गद्यांश के अनुसार कौन-सा वाक्य सही है, यह पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (4) — यह कला भी है और एक व्यवसाय भी है।**
गद्यांश में स्पष्ट लिखा है कि विज्ञापन "कला के साथ-साथ एक व्यवसाय भी बन गया है", जो सीधे इस विकल्प से मेल खाता है। शेष विकल्प गद्यांश के तथ्यों (आवश्यकता बढ़ रही है, सांस्कृतिक आयोजन भी विज्ञापन क्षेत्र में शामिल हैं) के विपरीत हैं।

**Q131.** प्राचीन काल में विज्ञापन का स्वरूप पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (2) — मुनादी द्वारा विज्ञापन करवाना।**
गद्यांश के अनुसार "ढिंढोरा पीटना, मुनादी करना ये सब विज्ञापन के ही रूप थे", जो प्राचीन काल के विज्ञापन के तरीकों को बताता है। पत्र-पत्रिका, टी.वी. और रेडियो आधुनिक/परवर्ती साधन हैं, जिनका उल्लेख गद्यांश में बाद के विकास के रूप में हुआ है।

**Q132.** "विज्ञापन के क्षेत्र में क्रान्ति आई" वाक्य का आशय पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (3) — विज्ञापन के क्षेत्र में अभूतपूर्व परिवर्तन हुआ है।**
'क्रान्ति' शब्द का अर्थ है बड़ा और अभूतपूर्व परिवर्तन, जो पुस्तकों के प्रकाशन और रेडियो के आविष्कार से विज्ञापन के क्षेत्र में आए बड़े बदलाव को दर्शाता है। यह विज्ञापन के घटने या सूचना न पहुँचने जैसे नकारात्मक भाव को नहीं दर्शाता।

**Q133.** विज्ञापन का सामान्य अर्थ पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (3) — लोगों तक सूचनाएँ पहुँचाना।**
गद्यांश की पहली पंक्ति में ही विज्ञापन को "किसी प्रकार की नयी सूचना को जन-जन तक पहुँचाना" बताया गया है, जो सीधे इस विकल्प से मेल खाता है। यह केवल विशिष्ट वर्ग तक सीमित या भ्रामक कार्य नहीं है, बल्कि व्यापक जनसंचार है।

**Q134.** प्रत्यय की दृष्टि से भिन्न शब्द पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (1) — ललित।**
'शोधित', 'उत्पादित' और 'निर्मित' — तीनों शब्दों में कृदंत प्रत्यय (क्रिया से बना विशेषण, "किया हुआ" के भाव वाला) 'इत' जुड़ा है, जबकि 'ललित' एक मूल गुणवाचक विशेषण है जिसमें ऐसा कोई क्रिया-आधारित प्रत्यय नहीं जुड़ा — इसलिए यह संरचना की दृष्टि से भिन्न है।

**Q135.** गद्यांश में विज्ञापन के किस स्वरूप का उल्लेख नहीं हुआ, यह पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (1) — भित्ति लेखन (दीवार लेखन)।**
गद्यांश में रेडियो, टी.वी., और पत्र-पत्रिकाओं का स्पष्ट उल्लेख है, परंतु 'भित्ति लेखन' (दीवारों पर विज्ञापन लिखना) शब्द कहीं नहीं आया — इसलिए यही एक विकल्प है जो पाठ से बाहर है।

**Q136.** मातृभाषा से शुरू कर क्रमशः अनेक भाषाएँ सीखने की नीति का नाम पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (2) — मातृभाषा आधारित बहुभाषावाद।**
यह वर्णन ठीक वैसा ही है जैसा 'मातृभाषा आधारित बहुभाषावाद' (Mother Tongue-Based Multilingualism) की अवधारणा में बताया जाता है — बच्चे अपनी मातृभाषा से शिक्षा शुरू करते हैं और धीरे-धीरे अन्य भाषाएँ जोड़ते जाते हैं। यह त्रिभाषा सूत्र जैसी किसी विशिष्ट भाषा-सूची की नीति नहीं, बल्कि व्यापक शैक्षणिक सिद्धांत है।

**Q137.** 'उत्पादक शब्द संपदा' (productive vocabulary) की सही परिभाषा पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (3) — वे शब्द जिनका प्रयोग हम लेखन और बोलने में करते हैं।**
उत्पादक शब्द संपदा वे शब्द हैं जिन्हें शिक्षार्थी सक्रिय रूप से बोलने और लिखने में स्वयं प्रयोग कर सकता है, न कि केवल पहचान सकता है। केवल सुनकर पहचानने वाले शब्द 'ग्राह्यशील शब्दावली' (receptive vocabulary) कहलाते हैं, जो इससे भिन्न श्रेणी है।

**Q138.** कक्षा में विषय-क्षेत्रों के लिए प्रयुक्त भाषा कौशल का नाम पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (2) — संज्ञानात्मक अकादमिक भाषायी निपुणता (CALP)।**
कक्षा में विभिन्न विषय-क्षेत्रों की पढ़ाई हेतु प्रयुक्त औपचारिक, अकादमिक भाषा 'Cognitive Academic Language Proficiency' कहलाती है, जो रोजमर्रा की बातचीत की भाषा से भिन्न और अधिक जटिल होती है। 'बुनियादी अन्त:वैयक्तिक संप्रेषण कौशल' (BICS) रोज़मर्रा की सामाजिक बातचीत के लिए होता है, अकादमिक विषय-शिक्षण के लिए नहीं।

**Q139.** 'नियम उन्मुखी भाषा निवेश' (rule-oriented/form-oriented language input) का अर्थ पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (4) — शब्द संपदा, उच्चारण और व्याकरण का प्रयोग।**
नियम/रूप उन्मुखी निवेश भाषा की संरचना पर बल देता है — जैसे शब्दावली, उच्चारण और व्याकरण, न कि सूचना या अर्थ पर। इसके विपरीत, विषय-वस्तु उन्मुखी निवेश भाषा के माध्यम से दी जाने वाली सूचना/जानकारी पर केंद्रित होता है।

**Q140.** दिए गए कथन में निहित व्याकरणिक ज्ञान का प्रकार पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (2) — घोषणात्मक ज्ञान (Declarative knowledge)।**
"नियमों के बारे में ज्ञान, उनका वर्णन करना और सीमित अभ्यास में प्रयोग करना" — यह नियमों को केवल जानने और बताने (knowing-that) पर केंद्रित है, वास्तविक धाराप्रवाह उपयोग (knowing-how) पर नहीं, इसलिए यह घोषणात्मक ज्ञान है। प्रक्रियात्मक ज्ञान इसके विपरीत वास्तविक, सहज भाषा-प्रयोग की क्षमता को दर्शाता है।

**Q141.** दिए गए कार्य (आधा पाठ पढ़कर सुनाना और साथी द्वारा लिखना, फिर भूमिका बदलना) का सही नाम पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (1) — पारस्परिक श्रुतलेख (Mutual dictation)।**
इस गतिविधि में दोनों शिक्षार्थी बारी-बारी से एक-दूसरे को पाठ का भाग बोलकर सुनाते हैं (dictate) और लिखते हैं, यानी श्रुतलेख की क्रिया परस्पर (mutual) रूप से दोनों दिशाओं में होती है — इसीलिए यह 'पारस्परिक श्रुतलेख' कहलाता है, न कि केवल एकतरफा अनुच्छेद श्रुतलेख।

**Q142.** 'सामग्री को शिक्षणशास्त्रीय रूप से अनुभव कर पाना' का अर्थ पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (4) — अध्यापक को शिक्षणशास्त्र स्वीकार करने के समर्थ बनाने हेतु सामग्री को शिक्षणशास्त्र में शामिल करना।**
'Pedagogical realization of materials' का अर्थ है कि शिक्षण सामग्री में ही शिक्षणशास्त्रीय दृष्टिकोण/रणनीतियाँ अंतर्निहित कर दी जाएँ, ताकि अध्यापक उस सामग्री का प्रयोग करते हुए स्वाभाविक रूप से उपयुक्त शिक्षणशास्त्र अपना सके। यह केवल रणनीतियों की सूची बनाने या कक्षा-गतिविधियाँ जोड़ने से आगे की बात है — सामग्री स्वयं शिक्षणशास्त्र को वहन करती है।

**Q143.** 'अधिगम के लिए आकलन' (Assessment for Learning) पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (3) — वह आकलन जिसकी अभिकल्पना शिक्षण अधिगम प्रक्रिया के दौरान विद्यार्थियों की प्रगति का पर्यवेक्षण करने और उनमें सुधार लाने के लिए की जाती है।**
'अधिगम के लिए आकलन' सतत, चल रही शिक्षण प्रक्रिया के भीतर ही होता है और इसका उद्देश्य तुरंत सुधार व फीडबैक देना है, न कि अंत में केवल ग्रेड देना। टर्म के अंत में या वर्ष के अंत में होने वाला आकलन 'अधिगम का आकलन' (Assessment of Learning) कहलाता है, जो इससे भिन्न श्रेणी है।

**Q144.** काव्य-शिक्षण के लिए अनुशंसित न किया जाने वाला कथन पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (2) — काव्य व्याकरण शिक्षण के लिए है।**
काव्य का मूल उद्देश्य आनंद देना, कल्पनाशीलता विकसित करना और भाषा के साथ रचनात्मक ढंग से कार्य करना है — व्याकरण के नियम सिखाने के औजार के रूप में काव्य का प्रयोग करना काव्य-शिक्षण की भावना के विपरीत और सीमित दृष्टिकोण है। शेष तीन कथन काव्य-शिक्षण के व्यापक रूप से स्वीकृत उद्देश्यों को दर्शाते हैं।

**Q145.** होटल मेन्यू पर बजट व प्राथमिकताओं संबंधी कार्य का नाम पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (4) — अध्ययन संलग्नता – विमर्श – प्रयोग (भाषा) [Study-Engage-Reflect-Use]।**
यह कार्य वास्तविक जीवन जैसी स्थिति (बजट में भोजन चुनना, प्राथमिकताएँ तय करना) में भाषा के सार्थक प्रयोग, चर्चा और विचार-विमर्श की माँग करता है, जो 'Study-Engage-Reflect-Use' ढाँचे से मेल खाता है, न कि केवल यांत्रिक अभ्यास (Present-Practice-Produce) से।

**Q146.** भाषा अर्जन की 'सहजात परिकल्पना' (innateness hypothesis) के बारे में सही कथन पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (2) — भाषा को उत्पन्न करने और भाषा अर्जन हेतु भाषा संकाय बच्चों की स्थायी निधि है (उन्हें सम्पन्न बनाती है)।**
सहजात परिकल्पना के अनुसार सभी मनुष्य जन्म से ही एक अंतर्निहित भाषा-संकाय (language faculty) लेकर पैदा होते हैं जो उन्हें स्वाभाविक रूप से भाषा अर्जित करने में सक्षम बनाती है — यह गहन अभ्यास/ड्रिल या केवल बाहरी वातावरण पर निर्भर होने की धारणा (व्यवहारवाद) के ठीक विपरीत है।

**Q147.** कौन-सी गतिविधि 'बॉटम-अप श्रवण गतिविधि' है, यह पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (3) — श्रुतलेख, क्लोज श्रवण, बहुवैकल्पिक प्रश्नों का प्रयोग, सकारात्मक और नकारात्मक कथनों की पहचान करना।**
बॉटम-अप श्रवण प्रक्रिया छोटी भाषिक इकाइयों (शब्द, ध्वनि, वाक्य-संरचना) को सुनकर पहचानने और उनसे अर्थ बनाने पर केंद्रित होती है — श्रुतलेख और क्लोज़ जैसी गतिविधियाँ ठीक इसी सूक्ष्म-स्तरीय प्रक्रिया का अभ्यास कराती हैं। सारांशीकरण और समग्र संदेश को समझना 'टॉप-डाउन' प्रक्रिया के उदाहरण हैं, जिसमें पृष्ठभूमि ज्ञान का प्रयोग समग्र अर्थ बनाने में होता है।

**Q148.** 'अधिगम अर्थ की खोज है' विचार का समर्थन करने वाला शैक्षिक दर्शन पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (4) — रचनावाद (Constructivism)।**
रचनावाद के अनुसार शिक्षार्थी स्वयं सक्रिय रूप से अर्थ खोजते और अपनी समझ का निर्माण करते हैं, इसीलिए अधिगम को उन वास्तविक मुद्दों से जोड़ना चाहिए जिनमें शिक्षार्थी सार्थकता खोजने का प्रयास कर रहे हैं। व्यवहारवाद (behaviourism) इसके विपरीत बाह्य उद्दीपन-अनुक्रिया पर आधारित है, अर्थ-निर्माण पर नहीं।

**Q149.** कक्षा 8 के लेखन कार्य (मंथन, पक्ष-विपक्ष तर्क) का नाम पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (1) — विमर्शात्मक लेखन (Discursive writing)।**
किसी विषय पर पक्ष और विपक्ष दोनों दृष्टिकोण प्रस्तुत करते हुए तर्कसंगत ढंग से विचार व्यक्त करना विमर्शात्मक (discursive) लेखन की पहचान है। वर्णनात्मक, निजी, या व्याख्यात्मक लेखन में इस प्रकार का पक्ष-विपक्ष तर्क-संरचना केंद्रीय नहीं होती।

**Q150.** संध्या के मशीन-लर्निंग संबंधी लेख पढ़ने के तरीके का नाम पहचानना।
**सही उत्तर: (2) — बारीकी से पठन (स्केनिंग)।**
संध्या विशिष्ट जानकारी प्राप्त करने के उद्देश्य से आलेख पढ़ रही है, जो स्कैनिंग (किसी विशेष सूचना को खोजने के लिए तेज़ी से पढ़ना) की पहचान है। सरसरी तौर पर पठन (स्किमिंग) इसके विपरीत सामान्य विषय-वस्तु का अवलोकन पाने के लिए होता है, विशिष्ट जानकारी खोजने के लिए नहीं।
