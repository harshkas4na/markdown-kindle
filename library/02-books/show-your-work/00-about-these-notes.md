# About These Notes

*Show Your Work! 10 Ways to Share Your Creativity and Get Discovered* is a 2014 book by Austin Kleon, the follow-up to his bestseller *Steal Like an Artist*. Where the first book was about taking influence from others, this one is about the reverse: how to put your own work out into the world so that people — an audience, collaborators, employers, customers — can find you.

**What this folder contains:** chapter-by-chapter study notes written in original words. These are summaries of the ideas, not the text of the book itself. If a chapter grabs you, buy the real thing — it's short, visual, and worth owning: [austinkleon.com/show-your-work](https://austinkleon.com/show-your-work/).

## The book in one paragraph

Most people think self-promotion is something you do *after* the work is done, and that it requires either genius or shamelessness. Kleon's argument is that neither is true. Instead of networking, be *findable*: share the process of your work openly and consistently, in small pieces, every day. Do that for years and an audience assembles itself around you — not because you asked for attention, but because you were generous with what you know and honest about how you work.

## The 10 chapters

1. **You Don't Have to Be a Genius** — creativity is a group phenomenon; join a "scenius" and contribute as an amateur.
2. **Think Process, Not Product** — the finished piece is only part of the value; the making of it is shareable material.
3. **Share Something Small Every Day** — daily dispatches beat occasional masterpieces.
4. **Open Up Your Cabinet of Curiosities** — your tastes and influences are content too; share what you love, with credit.
5. **Tell Good Stories** — work doesn't speak for itself; the story around it determines how it's valued.
6. **Teach What You Know** — teaching your process doesn't create competitors, it creates an audience.
7. **Don't Turn Into Human Spam** — share and *listen*; wanting attention without giving any is spam.
8. **Learn to Take a Punch** — putting work out means taking criticism; build the tolerance for it.
9. **Sell Out** — money and art aren't enemies; charging for your work is how you keep making it.
10. **Stick Around** — careers are marathons; don't quit, keep chaining projects together.
