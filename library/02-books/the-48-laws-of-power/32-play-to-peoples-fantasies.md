# Law 32: Play to People's Fantasies

**The idea in one line:** people respond more strongly to a compelling story about how things could be than to a sober account of how things actually are — appealing to aspiration usually outperforms appealing to accuracy.

## The psychology

Everyday reality is, for most people, some mixture of mundane and disappointing relative to what they'd like it to be. A vision that offers escape from that gap — a better outcome, a more exciting version of events, a flattering story about who someone could become — activates motivation and enthusiasm that plain factual description rarely does on its own. This isn't necessarily deception; it's an observation about which kind of message actually moves people to act, versus which kind is merely accurate.

## Where this is used constructively

Marketing, storytelling, leadership, and persuasion all lean on this heavily and mostly legitimately — selling a vision of a better future is how most meaningful collective effort gets organized in the first place, from a company's mission to a political campaign to a simple pitch for a new project. People rally around aspiration; they rarely rally around a spreadsheet, even an accurate one.

## Where this crosses into manipulation

The line is crossed when the fantasy being sold has no honest relationship to what can actually be delivered — false promises, deliberately inflated expectations, or appeals to fantasy specifically designed to obscure a bad underlying reality rather than to motivate genuine effort toward an achievable one. A fantasy that's later revealed as knowingly hollow does more damage to trust than a sober, less exciting pitch would have.

## Practical reading

The useful skill here is framing real possibilities in their most compelling, aspirational form — most true things can be described either dryly or vividly, and the vivid version isn't automatically dishonest just because it's more appealing. The discipline is making sure the underlying substance can actually support the appeal being made, so the fantasy being sold and the reality being delivered stay close enough together that trust survives contact with the outcome.
