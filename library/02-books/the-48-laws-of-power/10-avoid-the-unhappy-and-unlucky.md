# Law 10: Infection — Avoid the Unhappy and Unlucky

**The idea in one line:** emotional states and patterns of misfortune are contagious through proximity — spend enough time around chronic negativity or someone whose bad luck is really a pattern of bad decisions, and both tend to rub off.

## The psychology

Humans are heavily influenced by the emotional tone of the people around them — mood, outlook, and even risk tolerance sync up with whoever's nearby, largely without conscious awareness. This isn't mystical; it's a well-documented feature of how social groups regulate emotion. Spend consistent time with someone whose default state is grievance, pessimism, or crisis, and that becomes a baseline you're pulled toward, whether or not you intend it.

The "unlucky" half is sharper and easier to misread as superstition. It's not about bad luck as some external force — it's that people who repeatedly land in crisis, over and over, are frequently generating the crisis themselves through recognizable patterns: poor judgment under pressure, burning relationships, chronic short-term thinking. Proximity to that person means proximity to the second-order effects of their pattern, even if you personally did nothing wrong.

## Where this gets misapplied

Taken uncharitably, this law becomes an excuse to abandon people going through genuinely hard circumstances that aren't of their own making — that's a different situation entirely, and cutting people off for having a hard year is a poor and frankly cold reading of the principle. The distinction that matters is between someone facing misfortune and someone whose misfortune is a recurring, self-generated pattern they refuse to examine.

## Practical reading

The useful discipline is noticing patterns over single incidents. One bad stretch doesn't indict a person. A consistent, years-long pattern of chaos that always seems to be someone else's fault is a much stronger signal, and it's fair to weigh how much bandwidth you extend to relationships like that — not out of cruelty, but because your own capacity and outlook are a finite resource, and where you spend them compounds in one direction or the other.
