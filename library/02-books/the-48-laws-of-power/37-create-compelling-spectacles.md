# Law 37: Create Compelling Spectacles

**The idea in one line:** a striking, memorable image or moment communicates faster and lodges deeper than the same message delivered as plain explanation — attention gravitates toward the vivid and forgets the merely described.

## The psychology

Most information competes for limited attention, and the brain is disproportionately drawn to the visually or emotionally striking over the abstract or purely verbal. A message delivered as a memorable moment — a vivid demonstration, a striking visual, a well-staged event — gets processed and retained far more effectively than the identical message delivered as a paragraph of explanation. This is partly about attention capture and partly about memorability: spectacle creates a mental anchor that plain description doesn't.

## Where this is used constructively

Presentations, launches, brand moments, and pivotal announcements all lean on this — a product demonstrated in action says more, faster, than a spec sheet describing the same product; a well-designed visual moment communicates a message that a paragraph of text would take much longer to convey and would be far more likely to be skimmed or forgotten. This is close to good communication practice generally: show, don't just tell, when the stakes are high enough to justify the extra effort of staging it well.

## Where this becomes manipulative

Spectacle can also be used to substitute for substance rather than to communicate it — a striking presentation engineered to distract from a weak underlying product or a bad decision, relying on the fact that vivid moments are remembered longer than the careful scrutiny that might have caught the problem. The tell is usually a mismatch: does the spectacle illustrate something real, or is it doing the work that genuine substance should be doing instead?

## Practical reading

When something genuinely matters and deserves to be understood and remembered, it's worth the extra effort of finding a vivid, concrete way to communicate it rather than defaulting to plain explanation — a demonstration over a description, a specific story over an abstract claim. The discipline is making sure the vividness is illustrating something true, rather than being deployed to paper over something that wouldn't survive plain description.
