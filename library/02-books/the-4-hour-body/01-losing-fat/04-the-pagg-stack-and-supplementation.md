# The PAGG Stack and Supplementation

**The idea in one line:** a small, specific stack of four supplements, timed around meals, aimed at improving insulin sensitivity rather than acting as a stimulant or appetite suppressant.

## What PAGG stands for

**PAGG** is Ferriss's acronym for a four-supplement combination he pairs with the Slow-Carb Diet:

- **P — Policosanol**, a plant-derived compound explored for cardiovascular/cholesterol effects.
- **A — Alpha-lipoic acid (ALA)**, an antioxidant compound also studied for glucose metabolism effects.
- **G — Green tea flavanols (EGCG)**, the catechin compound in green tea linked to fat oxidation research.
- **G — Garlic extract**, included for blood lipid and insulin-related effects.

The stack is timed specifically around meals (particularly ones containing more carbohydrate) rather than taken as a general daily supplement, which matters to the mechanism being claimed.

## The claimed mechanism

The unifying argument isn't "burns fat directly" — it's **insulin sensitivity**: the claim is that this combination helps the body handle the glucose from a meal more efficiently, blunting the insulin spike and, by extension, the fat-storage signal that spike sends. This is consistent with the same blood-sugar framing used for the Slow-Carb Diet itself — PAGG is presented as a supporting lever on the same mechanism, not an independent fat-loss method.

## How Ferriss frames supplement claims generally

The book is unusually candid, for a supplement chapter, about the state of evidence: individual ingredient studies are cited, but the *combination* and dosing protocol is presented as his own N=1 and small informal group testing, not a peer-reviewed stack. He explicitly separates "here's a study on ingredient X" from "here's what I did and measured on myself" — consistent with the self-experimentation framing from the book's opening chapter. Readers are pointed toward checking with a doctor given interactions are possible, particularly for anyone on blood-thinning or blood-sugar medication, since several of these compounds affect those systems.

## Takeaways for practice

- Supplements here are framed as amplifying an existing diet strategy (insulin sensitivity), not as a standalone fix.
- Timing around meals — not blanket daily dosing — is the specific protocol claim, and it's the detail most generic "PAGG" summaries drop.
- Treat any stack like this as the highest-uncertainty protocol in the book and verify against current guidance and your own doctor before use — supplement research and product formulations both move faster than any one book's citations.
