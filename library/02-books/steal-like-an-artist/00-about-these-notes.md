# About These Notes

*Steal Like an Artist: 10 Things Nobody Told You About Being Creative* is Austin Kleon's 2012 debut, the book that came before *Show Your Work!*. Where the sequel is about putting your work out into the world, this one is about where work comes from in the first place — and Kleon's answer is blunt: nowhere original. Everything is remixed from what came before it; the only real choice is what you steal from, and how honestly you transform it.

**What this folder contains:** chapter-by-chapter study notes written in original words. These are summaries of the ideas, not the text of the book itself. If a chapter grabs you, the real thing is short, illustrated, and worth owning: [austinkleon.com/steal](https://austinkleon.com/steal/).

## The book in one paragraph

The romantic idea of the lone genius who invents something from nothing is a myth — every artist, writer, and thinker is built out of the influences they let in, digested, and combined. Kleon's advice isn't "don't worry about originality," it's the opposite: take influence seriously as a *practice*. Study your heroes closely enough to copy them on purpose, then let enough different influences collide that what comes out the other side is recognizably yours. The rest of the book is the practical scaffolding around that idea — how to work with your hands as well as a screen, why side projects matter, why a boring routine beats a chaotic one, and why fewer tools and options usually produce better work than more.

## The 10 chapters

1. **Steal Like an Artist** — nothing is original; the skill is choosing what to take from and transforming it honestly.
2. **Don't Wait Until You Know Who You Are to Get Started** — voice is a byproduct of doing the work, not a precondition for starting it.
3. **Write the Book You Want to Read** — make the thing you personally wish existed; you're the first, most reliable audience.
4. **Use Your Hands** — analog tools produce different raw material than a screen; keep a physical practice alive.
5. **Side Projects and Hobbies Are Important** — the work that pays and the work that matters most aren't always the same thing, and that's fine.
6. **The Secret: Do Good Work and Share It With People** — neither talent hidden away nor promotion of weak work goes anywhere; you need both halves.
7. **Geography Is No Longer Our Master** — physical scenes still matter, but the internet means you're no longer required to live in one to belong to it.
8. **Be Nice (The World Is a Small Town)** — creative fields are reputational and small; generosity compounds, and burned bridges stay burned.
9. **Be Boring (It's the Only Way to Get Work Done)** — a stable, unglamorous routine is what actually produces a large body of work over a career.
10. **Creativity Is Subtraction** — unlimited freedom paralyzes; real constraints force the interesting decisions.

## Where this fits

Read before *Show Your Work!* if you want the pair in publication order — this one is about input (what you take in and how you use it), the other is about output (how you put your own work back into the world).
