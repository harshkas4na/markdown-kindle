# Dynamic Fees & LP Fee Optimization

**218 hook projects** — the single largest category in the entire directory (~39% of all submissions touch this).

## The problem, explained simply

On Uniswap v2/v3, every pool has one fixed swap fee (e.g. 0.30%), set once when the pool is created and never changing no matter what's happening in the market. This creates a mismatch:

- **When the market is calm**, a 0.30% fee is often *too high* — it discourages ordinary trading, so the pool does less volume than it could, and liquidity providers (LPs — the people who deposit token pairs into the pool so others can trade against them) earn less than if fees were lower and volume higher.
- **When the market is volatile** (prices moving fast), a 0.30% fee is often *too low*. Here's why that matters: when the external "true" price of an asset (on Binance, Coinbase, etc.) moves faster than the Uniswap pool's price, an arbitrageur can trade against the pool to pull its price back in line — and pockets the difference as pure profit. That profit comes directly out of the LPs' pocket in the form of giving up tokens at a stale, no-longer-fair price. This specific loss has a name: **Loss-Versus-Rebalancing (LVR)**, formalized in the 2022 paper *"Automated Market Making and Loss-Versus-Rebalancing"* by Milionis, Moallemi, Roughgarden, and Zhang. It shows mathematically that LPs are structurally worse off than if they had just rebalanced their portfolio directly, and that the gap widens with volatility.

A static fee can't adapt to either situation. So a fixed-fee pool is always leaving money on the table for LPs in one direction or another.

## Why so many teams attack this exact problem

Because it's the most legible, most measurable pain point in the entire AMM design space — you can literally back-test "would a dynamic fee formula have earned LPs more money than the static 0.30% fee over this historical price data," and get a number. It's also the *headline feature* Uniswap v4 hooks were built to unlock (dynamic fees were explicitly cited by the Uniswap Foundation as a flagship hook use case), so nearly every cohort tries a version of it.

## Common approaches seen in this dataset

1. **Volatility-based fees** — raise the fee when recent price volatility (measured via rolling standard deviation, ATR, or an oracle feed) is high, lower it when calm. Simple, but many entries are near-identical implementations of this idea.
2. **Directional / asymmetric fees (Nezlobin's formula)** — named after a formula popularized by Dan Robinson/Alex Nezlobin's public research on Uniswap v4 hook design: charge a *higher* fee on trades that push the pool price further from a reference price (likely informed/arbitrage flow) and a *lower* fee on trades that push it back toward the reference price (likely "helpful," uninformed flow). This directly targets LVR rather than just volatility in general.
3. **Auction-managed AMMs (am-AMM)** — based on the academic "am-AMM" (auction-managed AMM) design: instead of a fixed formula, the right to set the pool's fee (and often capture MEV) for a time window is auctioned off to a "strategist" or "manager," who competes with others and pays rent back to LPs. Several entries (Auction Managed AMM, Chronus, AuctionPool, EigenLVR) build on this model.
4. **Oracle-referenced fees** — use an external price feed (Chainlink, Pyth, or a custom oracle) as the "true price" reference, and price the fee based on deviation between pool price and oracle price.
5. **ML/volatility-forecasting fees** — instead of reacting to volatility that already happened, some hooks try to *predict* near-term volatility with a model and set fees ahead of time (see also `ai-ml-hooks.md`).
6. **Idle-capital yield stacking** — a related but distinct idea: rather than (or in addition to) changing the fee, redeploy liquidity that's sitting "out of range" (not currently earning fees because the price moved outside the LP's chosen band) into a lending protocol like Aave or Morpho so it earns interest even when it isn't earning swap fees.

## Prior art / reference material

- Milionis, Moallemi, Roughgarden, Zhang — *"Automated Market Making and Loss-Versus-Rebalancing"* (2022) — the formal LVR framework nearly every "dynamic fee to protect LPs" hook is implicitly trying to reduce.
- am-AMM (auction-managed AMM) research popularized in the Uniswap v4 hook community, e.g. work referenced by Uniswap Labs / Paradigm on auction-based fee-setting.
- Nezlobin's directional fee concept — informal but widely cited within the Uniswap hook-builder community as a simple heuristic for approximating LVR-aware fees without a full auction mechanism.
- Uniswap v4 core docs on hooks and dynamic fees (`beforeSwap`/`afterSwap` fee override mechanism).
