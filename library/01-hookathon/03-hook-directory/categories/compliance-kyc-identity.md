# Compliance, KYC & Identity

**60 hook projects.**

## The problem, explained simply

Uniswap pools are, by default, permissionless — literally anyone with a wallet can trade in them, no questions asked. That's a core value proposition of DeFi, but it's also a legal problem for anyone who wants to bring regulated activity on-chain: banks, funds managing real-world assets, and any token that's legally classified as a security typically must, by law, verify who they're transacting with (KYC — Know Your Customer) and screen for banned counterparties (AML — Anti-Money Laundering). A standard Uniswap pool has no way to enforce "only verified/whitelisted wallets can trade this."

A related but distinct idea is **identity/reputation without necessarily involving a legal KYC process** — proving a wallet is controlled by a unique human (Proof of Personhood/Humanity), to prevent bots or a single actor from creating many wallets to game a system (e.g. an airdrop, a governance vote, or a "one discount per person" rule).

## Why so many teams attack this

Regulatory compliance is widely seen as the single biggest blocker to institutional and traditional-finance money entering DeFi at scale, so "compliant DEX infrastructure" is a perennially attractive pitch. It's also directly tied to the RWA narrative (see `stablecoins-rwa-tokenized-assets.md`) — you can't legally trade a tokenized bond or equity on a fully permissionless pool in most jurisdictions, so compliance hooks are a prerequisite for that whole category to work in the real world, not just as a hackathon demo.

## Common approaches seen in this dataset

1. **Whitelist/KYC-gated pools** — only wallets that have completed an off-chain or on-chain identity check (often via a third-party KYC provider or an attestation) can swap in the pool.
2. **On-chain compliant DEX infrastructure for institutions** — let banks/financial institutions spin up their own regulated DEX instance built on Uniswap v4 primitives (Levery).
3. **Proof of Personhood/Humanity integration** — combine a hook with an external identity-proof system to ensure one human = one set of trading privileges, reducing Sybil attacks (multiple fake identities controlled by one actor).
4. **Regulation-aware fee/discount structures** — apply different fees or access rules depending on a wallet's verified jurisdiction or accreditation status, mirroring real securities-law distinctions (e.g. accredited vs. non-accredited investors).
5. **On-/off-ramp compliance** — hooks positioned at the boundary between fiat and crypto (p2p fiat exchange, on-ramping services) where compliance obligations are typically strictest.

## Prior art / reference material

- ERC-3643 (T-REX) permissioned token standard — explicitly named in this dataset (also referenced in the RWA category) as a real, production compliance standard for security tokens.
- Traditional KYC/AML regulatory frameworks (e.g. the Bank Secrecy Act in the US, FATF's Travel Rule internationally) — the real-world legal backdrop nearly every hook in this category is implicitly designing against.
- Proof of Humanity / Worldcoin-style proof-of-personhood systems — the general category of "unique human verification" infrastructure several hooks integrate with or reference conceptually.
