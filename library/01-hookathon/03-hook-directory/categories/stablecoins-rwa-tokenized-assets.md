# Stablecoins, RWA & Tokenized Assets

**102 hook projects.**

## The problem, explained simply

Two related but distinct problems live in this bucket:

**1. Stablecoin depegs.** A stablecoin (USDC, USDT, DAI, etc.) is supposed to always trade at $1.00. A generic AMM pool doesn't know that — it treats a USDC/USDT pool the same way it treats an ETH/USDC pool, with the same fee curve and the same sensitivity to large trades. That's wasteful (stablecoin pairs should ideally trade near-flat, with minimal slippage) and also dangerous: if one stablecoin starts to "depeg" (its price genuinely drifts from $1.00, e.g. during a crisis like the USDC depeg in March 2023 after Silicon Valley Bank failed), a naive pool doesn't defend itself — it just lets arbitrageurs drain the pegged side.

**2. Real-World Assets (RWA) and tokenized assets.** This is the broader effort to represent real-world things — government bonds, private credit, real estate, carbon credits, even stocks — as on-chain tokens that can then be traded via an AMM. The problem: these assets usually come with legal and regulatory requirements (only accredited investors can buy X, only KYC'd wallets can hold Y) that a plain, permissionless Uniswap pool has no way to enforce, plus they're often genuinely illiquid in a way crypto-native tokens aren't, so naive AMM math (designed for liquid, freely tradeable tokens) doesn't fit them well.

## Why so many teams attack this

Stablecoins are the highest-volume, most "boring but essential" part of DeFi, so optimizing them has obvious real-world value. RWA tokenization is one of the most-hyped narratives in crypto over the last two years (BlackRock, Franklin Templeton and others tokenizing funds), so it's a natural target for builders trying to make something that looks institutionally relevant.

## Common approaches seen in this dataset

1. **Depeg-aware dynamic fees** — penalize trades that push a stablecoin pair further from 1:1 parity, and reward trades that restore parity (STAS Hook, SARM Protocol).
2. **Depeg insurance** — automatically detect a depeg event via oracle and pay out insurance to affected LPs or holders (Bastion).
3. **RWA pools with built-in compliance** — combine an RWA token with a hook that enforces KYC/whitelisting directly at the swap level, so a "permissionless" AMM can still legally gate who trades a regulated asset (Regulated DEX Hook, InfraFund, DebtSync Sentinel).
4. **Tokenized real-world cash flows** — bonds, private debt, carbon credits, and renewable-energy assets represented as tokens with a purpose-built pool (Bond Marketplace AMM, Unicarbon, InfraFund).
5. **Illiquid asset AMM math** — different curve or fee assumptions for assets that don't behave like liquid crypto tokens (real estate, private credit).

## Prior art / reference material

- The March 2023 USDC depeg (triggered by Silicon Valley Bank's collapse) — the real-world event most "depeg-aware" hooks implicitly design against.
- Curve Finance's StableSwap invariant — the widely-cited AMM curve design specifically built for low-slippage stable-asset trading (a different curve than Uniswap's constant-product x*y=k), referenced conceptually even where not directly implemented.
- ERC-3643 (T-REX) — a real, widely used token standard for permissioned/compliant security tokens, explicitly named by at least one project in this dataset (Regulated DEX Hook) as the compliance layer for RWA tokens.
- Institutional RWA tokenization efforts (e.g. BlackRock's BUIDL fund, Franklin Templeton's on-chain fund) — the market backdrop motivating "compliant liquidity for real-world assets" as a category.
