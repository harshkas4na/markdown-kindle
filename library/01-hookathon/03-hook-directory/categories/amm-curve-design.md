# AMM Curve Design

**35 hook projects.**

## The problem, explained simply

Uniswap's default AMM math is the "constant product" formula, x * y = k — the product of the two token quantities in the pool must always stay the same. This one formula is simple and works reasonably well for a generic pair of volatile assets, but it isn't optimal for every situation:

- For **stable assets that should trade near 1:1** (two stablecoins, or a liquid-staking token and its underlying), the constant-product curve gives much more slippage than necessary — a **Constant Sum** curve (x + y = k, i.e. always exactly 1:1) would give zero slippage, but only works if the assets truly never diverge in value, which is a real risk (see depegs, `stablecoins-rwa-tokenized-assets.md`).
- For **value-weighted baskets of multiple assets** (like an index fund), neither pure formula is right — you want a curve that maintains fixed value-weight ratios across more than two assets.
- For **volatility-targeting or leveraged exposure**, you might want a curve whose behavior tracks an entirely different mathematical target (like a targeted implied volatility) rather than a fixed product/sum relationship.

Hooks let developers override the pool's pricing behavior enough to approximate these alternative curve shapes on top of Uniswap v4's infrastructure, instead of needing an entirely separate AMM codebase (as Curve Finance, Balancer, and others built pre-v4).

## Why so many teams attack this

This is one of the more academically "pure" areas of AMM design — it's well-studied in DeFi research (Curve, Balancer, and Uniswap itself all publish math-heavy documentation on their respective curve choices), so it attracts more technically-inclined builders who want to implement known curve math as a hook rather than invent a new financial product from scratch.

## Common approaches seen in this dataset

1. **Constant Sum Market Maker (CSMM) hooks** — approximate a 1:1, zero-slippage curve for pegged-asset pairs, generally combined with safeguards for when the peg breaks.
2. **Hybrid/blended curves** — combine constant-product behavior for normal conditions with constant-sum-like behavior near the peg, switching dynamically (similar in spirit to Curve Finance's StableSwap invariant, though not always implemented identically).
3. **Value-weighted/index-style pools** — maintain a fixed value-weight ratio across a basket, roughly analogous to Balancer's weighted-pool design, rebuilt as a v4 hook.
4. **Volatility-targeting curves** — instead of pricing purely off supply ratios, target a specific implied volatility level and adjust accordingly (Implied Volatility Targeting AMM).

## Prior art / reference material

- Uniswap v2 whitepaper — the original constant-product (x*y=k) formula that is the default and reference point for every alternative curve in this category.
- Curve Finance's StableSwap whitepaper — the widely cited hybrid curve design purpose-built for low-slippage stable-asset trading.
- Balancer's weighted-pool math (from the Balancer whitepaper) — the reference design for multi-asset, value-weighted pools.
