# MEV & LVR Protection

**126 hook projects.**

## The problem, explained simply

MEV stands for **Maximal Extractable Value** — the profit a block producer (or a bot that pays the block producer) can extract by choosing *which* transactions go in a block and in *what order*, beyond just the normal transaction fee. On a DEX like Uniswap, the classic version of this is the **sandwich attack**: a bot sees your pending "buy" transaction in the public mempool, buys the same asset right before you (pushing the price up), lets your trade execute at the now-worse price, then immediately sells (pocketing the difference). You end up with a worse price than you should have gotten, and you never even know a bot was involved.

A related but distinct problem is **LVR (Loss-Versus-Rebalancing)** — see `dynamic-fees-lp-optimization.md` for the full explanation. In short: whenever the external market price moves, arbitrage bots race to correct the AMM's price and pocket the gap, and that gap is money that would otherwise have stayed with liquidity providers. LVR is really "structural, unavoidable MEV that LPs bleed to arbitrageurs by design," as opposed to sandwich attacks which target traders directly.

Both problems exist because Ethereum (and most EVM chains) have a **public mempool** — pending transactions are visible to everyone before they're confirmed, so anyone running a bot can see your trade coming and act on it first.

## Why so many teams attack this

MEV extraction from Uniswap has historically been worth hundreds of millions of dollars, is well documented (Flashbots' MEV-explore, and academic literature), and is *the* most-cited weakness of AMMs. Hooks are a natural place to add MEV mitigation because `beforeSwap`/`afterSwap` hooks can inspect the transaction and react before value is extracted, whereas in v2/v3 the only tools available were external (private mempools/relays like Flashbots Protect).

## Common approaches seen in this dataset

1. **Dynamic/directional fees that tax likely-arbitrage flow** — see the dynamic fees category; the same lever that improves LP yield in calm-vs-volatile markets can also be aimed specifically at making sandwich attacks and arbitrage less profitable.
2. **Auction-based MEV redistribution (am-AMM style)** — instead of trying to *prevent* MEV, let a "manager" bid for the right to arbitrage the pool, and give that bid revenue back to LPs (EigenLVR, LVR Auction Hook, Shield-Auction-Hook, AuctionPool). This is directly inspired by the am-AMM literature and by "MEV redistribution" experiments like MEV-Share/MEV-Boost in the broader Ethereum ecosystem.
3. **Encrypted/private order flow (FHE)** — use Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE, via providers like Fhenix) so the contents or direction of a trade stay hidden until after it's committed, denying bots the information they need to sandwich you (ShadowSwap, CipherFlow, ZK-JIT Liquidity Hook, ShadowBrain).
4. **Coincidence of Wants (CoW) / order batching** — instead of routing every trade individually through the AMM (where it's visible and attackable), match opposing trades against each other first ("Alice wants to sell X for Y, Bob wants to buy X with Y — just match them directly") and only send the unmatched remainder to the pool. This is the same idea behind CoW Protocol / CoW Swap in mainstream DeFi. (EigenMatch CoW Hook, RecorrHook, CoW-tagged entries.)
5. **Priority-fee-aware fees** — detect when a transaction is paying an unusually high priority fee (a signal that it's probably a bot racing to be first, i.e. probably MEV) and charge it more.
6. **TWAMM (Time-Weighted Average Market Maker)** — break a large trade into many small pieces executed gradually over time rather than as one big transaction, which reduces both price impact and the incentive to snipe it.

## Prior art / reference material

- Milionis, Moallemi, Roughgarden, Zhang — *"Automated Market Making and Loss-Versus-Rebalancing"* (2022).
- Flashbots research on MEV, MEV-Boost, and MEV-Share — the primary applied research body documenting sandwich attacks and MEV markets on Ethereum.
- am-AMM (auction-managed AMM) design for redistributing MEV/arbitrage rent to LPs.
- CoW Protocol / CoW Swap — batch auction and "coincidence of wants" matching as an MEV-resistant trading design.
- Paradigm's public research and blog posts on TWAMM (time-weighted average market maker), which originated as a Paradigm research concept before v4 hooks made it directly implementable.
- Fhenix and other FHE-on-EVM projects — the encryption layer several "private trading" hooks build on top of.
