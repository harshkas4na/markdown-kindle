# Trading Rewards & Gamification

**131 hook projects.**

## The problem, explained simply

A brand-new token or pool has a chicken-and-egg problem: traders won't show up if there's no liquidity/volume, and liquidity providers won't show up if there's no trading activity to earn fees from. This is the **cold-start problem** in DeFi, and it's been "solved" for years the crude way: token projects hand out extra reward tokens (liquidity mining / "yield farming") to anyone who trades or provides liquidity, hoping the activity becomes self-sustaining before the reward budget runs out.

This category is the hook-native version of that same instinct, expanded with game-design techniques (points, tiers, streaks, leaderboards, referrals, lotteries) borrowed from consumer apps to make participating in a pool feel more like a game with a payoff, rather than a plain financial decision. A separate but related idea in this bucket is **copy trading** — letting less experienced users automatically mirror a successful trader's moves — which is a reward/engagement mechanism aimed at attracting less sophisticated capital into a pool.

## Why so many teams attack this

It's a "growth marketing" problem more than a pure technical problem, so it's approachable for any skill level, extremely visible/demoable (leaderboards and badges make for a good hackathon demo), and directly ties into a metric every project cares about: getting people to actually use what you built.

## Common approaches seen in this dataset

1. **Points / tiers / badges for trading volume** — track a wallet's cumulative volume or LP contribution on-chain and unlock tiers, discounts, or token rewards as thresholds are crossed.
2. **Referral programs** — reward both a user and whoever referred them for trading activity, ported from classic Web2 growth loops.
3. **Gamefi mechanics: streaks, lotteries, tournaments** — daily check-in streaks, raffle draws funded by pool fees, or PnL-based trading tournaments (PoolArena, TradeBattle, HooLotto, StreakFi).
4. **Copy trading** — let a wallet subscribe to mirror another wallet's swaps automatically (Mirror, LP Hub's copy-trading feature).
5. **Fee discounts as loyalty rewards** — instead of a separate reward token, simply reduce the swap fee for high-volume or loyal traders directly inside the hook.
6. **Social/creator-economy token launches** — several entries (Blueprint, Steelo.io, Fanio) tie trading rewards to creator/fan economies, letting a creator's community trade a personal or event token with built-in incentive loops.

## Prior art / reference material

- "Liquidity mining" / "yield farming," first popularized broadly by Compound's COMP distribution and Synthetix in 2020 — the foundational Web3 pattern nearly every reward hook here is a variant of.
- Standard Web2 growth-hacking mechanics (referral loops, streaks, tiers) — borrowed wholesale from consumer mobile-app design, not DeFi-specific research.
- Copy-trading platforms in TradFi/crypto (eToro, and various on-chain copy-trading protocols) — the general precedent for "mirror another trader's positions automatically."
