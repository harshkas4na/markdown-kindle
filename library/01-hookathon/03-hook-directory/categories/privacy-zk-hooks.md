# Privacy & ZK Hooks

**24 hook projects.**

## The problem, explained simply

Every detail of a normal Ethereum transaction — what you're trading, how much, and often your entire wallet history — is public forever. This has two consequences that hooks in this category try to fix:

1. **It enables MEV** (see `mev-lvr-protection.md`) — bots can see your pending trade before it's confirmed and act on that information against you.
2. **It's simply bad for privacy** — institutions in particular don't want competitors seeing their trading strategy or position sizes, and individuals may not want their entire financial history permanently public.

Two different cryptographic tools are used to address this in the dataset:

- **Zero-Knowledge proofs (zk-SNARKs)** — let you *prove* a statement is true (e.g. "I have at least 5,000 USDC in this pool" or "I am a unique verified human") without revealing the underlying data itself.
- **Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE)** — lets a smart contract *compute directly on encrypted data* without ever decrypting it, so a hook can, for example, match or process trades whose direction and size stay encrypted the entire time, only becoming visible (if ever) after execution.

## Why so many teams attack this

FHE-on-EVM projects like Fhenix, and various zk-proof toolkits (e.g. Brevis), were actively promoted integrations within the UHI program, so builders had ready-made tools to plug into. Privacy is also a naturally compelling pitch because it directly counters the most commonly cited weakness of public blockchains.

## Common approaches seen in this dataset

1. **Encrypted order flow / confidential swaps** — hide the direction and/or size of a trade using FHE until after it's committed, denying front-runners the information they need (ShadowSwap, CipherFlow, Vaultage, ZK-JIT Liquidity Hook).
2. **Zero-knowledge proof-of-contribution** — prove a wallet meets some threshold (e.g. minimum liquidity provided) without revealing exact balances, unlocking benefits elsewhere without full transparency (Liquidity Rewards via Brevis).
3. **Mixer-style privacy** — directly adapt privacy-pool designs (like Tornado Cash) into an AMM context, breaking the on-chain link between depositor and withdrawer (Tornado Hook, Vortex Protocol).
4. **Confidential attestations for gamified/loyalty systems** — combine privacy tech with the reward mechanisms discussed in `trading-rewards-gamification.md` so participation can be verified without exposing full activity history (StreakFi).

## Prior art / reference material

- Tornado Cash — the best-known zk-based mixer design; directly referenced/adapted by at least one project (Tornado Hook) in this dataset. (Note: Tornado Cash itself has faced significant regulatory/sanctions scrutiny in the US — a useful real-world caveat for anyone building in this space, referenced in the directory's own legal disclaimer.)
- Fhenix — an FHE-enabled EVM-compatible chain, the most commonly integrated FHE provider across this dataset's "encrypted trading" hooks.
- Brevis — a zk-coprocessor/proof infrastructure provider referenced for proof-of-liquidity-contribution use cases.
- General zk-SNARK literature (e.g. Groth16, PLONK proof systems) — the underlying cryptographic primitives beneath any "zk-SNARK" tagged project, though most entries don't specify which proof system they use.
