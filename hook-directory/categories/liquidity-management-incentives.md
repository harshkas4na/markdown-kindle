# Liquidity Management & Incentives

**153 hook projects.**

## The problem, explained simply

On Uniswap v3/v4, liquidity providers don't just deposit tokens into one giant pool — they choose a **price range** ("I'll provide liquidity only between $1,800 and $2,200 per ETH"). This is called **concentrated liquidity**, and it lets LPs earn more fees per dollar deposited *while the price stays in their chosen range*. But the moment the price moves outside that range, two bad things happen at once:

1. Their liquidity stops earning any swap fees at all — it just sits there, doing nothing, while still being 100% exposed to price risk. This is "idle" or "out-of-range" capital.
2. Getting back "in range" requires either the market moving back, or the LP manually withdrawing and re-depositing at a new range (paying gas, and needing to actively watch the market).

Most LPs are not full-time traders. They can't babysit a position all day, so a large fraction of liquidity deposited into Uniswap sits out of range, earning nothing, at any given time. Separately, protocols and token projects that *want* deep liquidity for their token (so people can trade it without huge price impact) have to design an incentive program from scratch to attract LPs — there's no standard, plug-and-play answer to "how do I bootstrap liquidity for a new pool."

## Why so many teams attack this

Idle/out-of-range capital is one of the most quantifiable inefficiencies in DeFi — you can literally measure the dollar-hours of liquidity sitting unused. It's also a problem every single new token/pool launch faces (the "cold start" liquidity bootstrapping problem), so it recurs across almost every cohort in a slightly different flavor.

## Common approaches seen in this dataset

1. **Auto-rebalancing / auto-compounding vaults** — a hook (often combined with an ERC-4626 vault) that automatically moves an LP's range back to where the price currently is, and/or reinvests earned fees, without the LP doing anything manually.
2. **Route idle liquidity into lending markets** — when a position goes out of range, automatically deposit that idle capital into a lending protocol (Aave, Morpho) so it earns interest instead of sitting idle, and pull it back when the price re-enters range. (Idle Liquidity Yield Earning Hook, Hedgehog ALM, Unicord, AlphaEngine, and many "NoOp" hooks.)
3. **Just-In-Time (JIT) liquidity** — instead of sitting in the pool permanently, liquidity is deployed *only* for the instant a large trade executes (to capture the fee) and withdrawn immediately after, maximizing capital efficiency for sophisticated LPs.
4. **Liquidity mining / points programs** — reward LPs with extra tokens or points for depositing, often tiered by amount or duration, to bootstrap a new pool's depth (a mechanism as old as "yield farming" from DeFi Summer 2020, now rebuilt as a hook rather than a separate rewards contract).
5. **Native, protocol-level incentive layers** — instead of every project building its own rewards contract, provide a general-purpose "incentive campaign" hook that any pool can plug into.

## Prior art / reference material

- Uniswap v3 whitepaper — introduced concentrated liquidity and the range-based LP model that creates the "out of range" problem in the first place.
- ERC-4626 ("Tokenized Vault Standard") — the standard interface many "auto-deploy idle liquidity to a lender" hooks build their vault logic on top of.
- Yearn Finance / auto-compounding vault designs — the general pattern ("deposit once, let a strategy contract manage it") that many rebalancer/vault hooks borrow from, applied specifically to concentrated liquidity ranges.
- "Liquidity mining" as popularized by Compound/Synthetix in 2020 DeFi Summer — the incentive-token-for-deposits pattern reused here as a hook-native feature.
